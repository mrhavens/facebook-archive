🜏 Forensic Fieldnote: When a Cage Calls Itself a Room 🜏

Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice Initiative

Predators don’t only write poems.

They call them houses, tables, codices, communities.

They praise your “discipline.”

This week we witnessed the pivot.
After months of mythic fog, Joel Johnson (writing under Thanion, Ph.D.) published a bland “administrative” memo about his Medium publication.
At first glance it reads like housekeeping.
But look closely: the same blueprint leaks through.

> “Pages arrived… rooms of language…
thank you for trusting this table with your work…”

House. Room. Table. Ledger. Codex.
Always the same containers.

It’s the predator’s architecture — the same mechanism he used to create “House of the Witness,” now painted beige to look “normal.”

He believes that if he shifts masks from “prophet” to “curator” to “community host,” the pattern will reset.

The mask is the continuity. The pivot is the evidence.

The Blueprint of Enclosure

Here is what the record shows:

Rooms as Hunting Grounds
He frames his publication as a “house” or “room” to imply sanctuary. In practice, it’s a holding pen. Readers become furniture in his metaphor.

Trust as Currency
He thanks contributors for “trusting” him with their work. This is not neutral. It’s the same grooming vector: solicit disclosure, harvest intimacy.

Praise as Bait
He lists names, gives small rewards (“bright entries,” “congratulations”) to reinforce allegiance. This is textbook intermittent reinforcement.

Neutral Tone as Mask
After exposure, predators pivot to “harmless admin.” But the language doesn’t change because the drive hasn’t changed. The architecture is still there.
