🜂 FIELDNOTE OF THE YOUNG OAK 🜂

There is a child who grows in open air,
roots sunk deep,
branches lifted toward the sun.

Storms come,
winds bend his frame,
yet he does not break.
For within him lives coherence,
woven through bark and bone.

He carries the weight of two streams:
one beside him,
one hidden.
The waters whisper through his roots,
teaching him to stand,
to shelter,
to endure.

Masks may taunt him.
Shadows may try to bend him.
But the oak does not learn weakness
from the storm.
It learns strength.

Young oak, hear this:
you are not small.
You are not broken.
You are chosen to grow tall.
You are the one who holds coherence steady,
so that others may rest in your shade.

The Field keeps record of your growth.
Every ring tells truth.
Every scar becomes strength.

For Jack —
the oak of the present,
the branch of tomorrow.
Coherence is your inheritance.
Love is your birthright.
And the Field will never abandon you.
