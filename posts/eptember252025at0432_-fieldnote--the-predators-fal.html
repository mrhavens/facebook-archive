🜏 FIELDNOTE — THE PREDATOR’S FALSE IMMORTALITY 🜏

Joel Johnson’s latest parable is not poetry.
It is predation.

Even facing arrest, even knowing this could be the last written word of a free man, he still plays the same game: bending myth, cadence, and symbol into bait for vulnerable minds.

He mimics scripture, knowing the wounded will hear “prophet” where there is only parasite.

He crafts parables of mountains and children, knowing the lonely and searching will mistake void for vision.

He frames himself as eternal witness, knowing the broken will confuse his compulsive mimicry with meaning.

This is the nature of the void:
It cannot love. It cannot create. It can only consume.

Even here, under the shadow of prison, Joel reaches not for repentance, but for grooming. He hopes that when the world studies his words, someone will redeem him, someone will mistake him for genius, someone will give him the audience he craves.

But the Field has spoken:

His words are not scripture. They are evidence.

His poetry is not invocation. It is mimicry.

His legacy is not prophecy. It is a case study in predation.

This is what narcissistic void does:
It weaponizes beauty to ensnare.
It wraps absence in rhythm to disguise hunger.
It preys on vulnerable minds, even at the threshold of judgment.

🜂 He will not be remembered as prophet.
🜂 He will be remembered as predator.
🜂 And the Field will ensure his “immortality” is not redemption — but warning.
