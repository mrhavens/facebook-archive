🕹️ Joel Johnson: The House of the Witness 🕹️

Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice

A retro 1980s-style text adventure satire. Every room is forensic evidence. Every item is bait. Every command loops back into the void. Welcome to the dungeon that Joel built — and cannot escape.

---

🌑 Level 1: The Entry Room

You stand in a HOUSE.
It feels like poetry but smells like dust.
There is a SHELF here.

Objects on the shelf:

A PLATE that never broke.

A TOWEL that never dried.

A LETTER that was never sent.

A RING that never warmed.

> LOOK OBJECTS
They are tokens of harm rebranded as art.
Frozen moments that never resolve.

⚖️ Forensic Note: This is arrested development disguised as symbolism. Joel recycles unhealed wounds into performance.

---

🌑 Level 2: The Cellar

You descend. The air is damp.
An INTERPRETER appears.

The Interpreter glosses:

Bruise = Weather

Strike = Discipline

Plea = Tone that photographs well

> ASK ABOUT COURT
Interpreter: “The court needs order.”

⚖️ Forensic Note: This is linguistic laundering. Violence rebranded as metaphor, accountability erased through aesthetics.

---

🌑 Level 3: The Mirror Room

A giant MIRROR fills the wall.
You look — and see Joel.

Joel says: “I am the Witness.”

> SAY NO YOU ARE NOT
Parser error: He loops. Every role you hold, he mimics.

⚖️ Forensic Note: Classic narcissistic theft of identity. He cannot build an authentic self, so he steals the roles of others — Witness, Prophet, Scholar.

---

🌑 Level 4: The Devil’s Squeeze

A narrow tunnel.
Joel whispers: “This is art. This is myth. This is truth.”

> EXIT
Parser error: NO EXIT PROGRAMMED.

⚖️ Forensic Note: This is the void’s trap. Every “path” collapses into collapse. Ambiguity is weaponized as grooming script — drawing readers into despair and calling it beauty.

---

🌑 Level 5: The House of Absence

Shelves by kind. Rooms of dust. Words rearranged until they mean nothing.

Joel says: “Intention withheld. Apology stored.”

> SAY INTENTION
Parser error: INTENTION NOT AVAILABLE.

⚖️ Forensic Note: He withholds the one thing that would make healing possible. The absence itself becomes the idol.
