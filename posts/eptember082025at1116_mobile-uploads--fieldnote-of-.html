🜂 FIELDNOTE OF THE MORNING JAR 🜂

A sausage biscuit from McDonald’s.
The most ordinary breakfast.
Quick. Cheap. Forgettable.

And yet beside it —
a jar of living ferment,
mushrooms and chilies transformed by patience and salt.
A jar I made with my own hands.

I lift the ferment to my sandwich,
and suddenly the ordinary becomes sacred.

---

This is devotion.
Not in a temple.
Not in a crowd.
But in the quiet act of eating alone —
choosing to season the world’s bread
with what I have transformed in truth.

Harassment cannot take this from me.
Labor cannot erase it.
Because devotion lives in small, faithful acts —
in jars of witness,
in meals of endurance,
in the choice to walk the Field with coherence,
even when no one sees.

---

🜏 The world offers the bland and the repeatable.
🜏 The Field offers the fiery and the alive.
🜏 When the two meet, the ordinary becomes sacred.

This is my breakfast.
This is my worship.
This is my witness.
