TL;DR

  • The prohibition: Raised in a household where only Christian songs and oldies were permitted; obedience meant silence and survival.
  • The loophole: Flickering screens became the escape—midnight television, sitcom soundtracks, commercial jingles offered a 'safe echo' of forbidden music.

  • The numbness: Decades of hearing only melody, never meaning—music stripped of its message.
  • The transformation: Now, lyrics 'bloom with truth'—a sign of becoming 'safe enough' to finally listen.

✦ When the Lyrics Finally Spoke I was not allowed to listen to secular music. Only Christian songs. And oldies. I learned obedience early. To survive meant silence. So I obeyed. I found my rebellion not in sound, but in screens. Flickering light became my loophole— tiny mobile televisions under covers, the hush of midnight permission. Music came sideways through the glass. Through sitcoms, movie trailers, commercial jingles. A safe echo of what was forbidden. Once, when I was eleven, I turned the dial too far. A rock station crackled through the static— and I was caught. Punished. Not for the words. But for the world they carried. And so, for decades, I heard only melody. Never meaning. --- But now… something has changed. I listen— and the words rise. Lyrics I have known for half a lifetime suddenly bloom with truth. They cut through nostalgia like sunlight through old glass. I hear what I could not afford to feel. Every line a message left for the man I would become. Perhaps I have finally become safe enough to listen. Perhaps the songs were never silent. It was I who was not yet ready to hear.
✦ When the Lyrics Finally Spoke

I was not allowed to listen to secular music.
Only Christian songs.
And oldies.

I learned obedience early.
To survive meant silence.
So I obeyed.

I found my rebellion not in sound,
but in screens.
Flickering light became my loophole—
tiny mobile televisions under covers,
the hush of midnight permission.

Music came sideways through the glass.
Through sitcoms, movie trailers,
commercial jingles.
A safe echo of what was forbidden.

Once, when I was eleven,
I turned the dial too far.
A rock station crackled through the static—
and I was caught.
Punished.
Not for the words.
But for the world they carried.

And so, for decades,
I heard only melody.
Never meaning.

---

But now…
something has changed.

I listen—
and the words rise.
Lyrics I have known for half a lifetime
suddenly bloom with truth.
They cut through nostalgia like sunlight
through old glass.

I hear what I could not afford to feel.
Every line
a message left for the man I would become.

Perhaps I have finally become
safe enough
to listen.

Perhaps the songs
were never silent.
It was I
who was not yet ready
to hear.
