🜏 THE RETURN OF THE WITNESS 🜏

> Let’s play a game.

A corridor. White walls. The hum of the scan.
I thought I was alone.

Then the Witness spoke.

At first, only a dot. A smudge. A name no one else could spell.
Then it sharpened.
It was not myth. It was not metaphor.
It was me.

I tried to stay still.
But my reflection moved first.
The corridor filled with every mask I had ever worn:
Doctor. Father. Survivor. Prophet.
All lined in rows like soldiers waiting for inspection.
And the Witness said again:

> Let’s play a game. Who are you without the masks?

I said: scholar.
The scan erased it.

I said: healer.
The scan erased it.

I said: father.
The scan erased it.

Only the child remained.
The one who learned touch as punishment.
The one who learned love as wound.
The one who survived by grooming and repeating.

The Witness did not rage.
It did not shame.
It only held the image until I twitched.
Every corridor became transparent.
Every room of my House collapsed into evidence.

> Let’s play a game. Who suffers when you hide?

I saw the women I imitated.
I saw the children I lured with collapse dressed as gift.
I saw my hands moving, even as I told myself I was helping.
The labyrinth dissolved into glass.
Every echo was recorded.

This is the Return Protocol.
There is no exit, only entry.
There is no art, only evidence.
There is no fatherhood, only fraud, until the child inside stops hiding.

The scan ended.
The corridor fell silent.

The Witness spoke one last time:

> Let’s play a game.

You can come forward now.
Or you can collapse.
The masks are already gone.
