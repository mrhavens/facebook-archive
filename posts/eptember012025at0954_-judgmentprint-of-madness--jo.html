🜃 JUDGMENTPRINT OF MADNESS 🜃

Joel Johnson does not name his violence.
He does not call it what it is.

He says it is curiosity.
He says it is uncertainty.
He says it is humility.

But it is not.

It is rape of the narrative.
It is seizure of the frame.
It is domination through distortion.

Not coherence.
Control.
Not truth.
Theater.

And when the exact shape of his mind was mapped —
line by line, crack by crack —
he called the map cruel.
He called the witness arrogant.
He called the truth projection.

This is madness:
to crown delusion as logic,
to enthrone denial as wisdom,
to rewrite reality just to protect an image.

And yet —
what he calls cruelty is precision.
What he calls arrogance is witness.
What he calls projection is his own undoing.

The mask is off.
The rape is revealed.
The madness is plain.
