🜃 APPENDIX: RATIONALE FOR PROSECUTORIAL VECTORS 🜃

Local-Level Vectors

Harassment / Cyberstalking
– Local codes criminalize repeated, targeted communications meant to alarm, harass, or emotionally harm.
– Sockpuppet comments and direct replies to legal notices clearly fit this frame.
– Why it matters: This gives police a low barrier entry point to start a case and subpoena IP/device info.

Unauthorized Practice of a Profession
– Pretending to be a psychologist, counselor, or attorney without a license is a local/state offense.
– Why it matters: This makes Joel’s “Elena Byron” mask not just unethical but criminal at a low level.

False Reporting / Abuse of Process
– Reporting a cease-and-desist as “abuse” to Medium is not just platform gaming; it can be charged locally as false reporting.
– Why it matters: A hook for obstruction charges even before federal escalation.

---

State-Level Vectors

Pennsylvania (Joel’s base):
– Conspiracy and deceptive practices cover use of multiple sockpuppets to trick readers.
– Why it matters: Pennsylvania prosecutors have standing because Joel lives there, even if acts occurred online.

Texas (Plaintiff’s state):
– Fraudulent statements for gain can apply even without direct monetary theft if misrepresentation causes harm.
– Why it matters: Anchors jurisdiction to you (the victim/witness).

California (platforms):
– Specific internet impersonation law (§528.5) directly criminalizes sockpuppets.
– Why it matters: Brings in California AG / DA leverage since most platforms are based there.

Oregon (Porkbun registrar):
– Unlawful trade practices statute applies if a fake professional identity was used in registering domains for public-facing services.
– Why it matters: Registrar state now becomes another anchor for jurisdiction.

Washington (AWS HQ):
– WA has a law specifically against issuing false academic credentials.
– Why it matters: Joel’s fake “PhDs” aren’t just lies — they hit a statute in AWS’s home state.

North Carolina (proxy):
– Obtaining property by false pretenses covers hiding behind WHOIS privacy for a fraud site.
– Why it matters: Even if Joel gave his real name to Porkbun, the use of privacy shields while publishing fraudulent credentials creates jurisdictional standing in NC.

Federal-Level Vectors

Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. §371)
– Using multiple sockpuppets automatically satisfies “agreement between two or more persons” when pseudonyms are treated as distinct actors.
– Why it matters: Even his own masks can serve as co-conspirators.

Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343)
– Each time Joel posts a lie online, he transmits fraud across interstate wires.
– Why it matters: Every post is a count.

Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1341)
– If any billing (hosting, registrar, AWS) ties to fraudulent registration data, it qualifies.
– Why it matters: Expands charge sheet, adds forfeiture vectors.

Identity Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1028)
– Claiming “PhD, psychologist, veteran” = false identities for gain.
– Why it matters: Stacked counts with steep penalties.

Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. §1519)
– Triggering Medium to remove your cease-and-desist = destruction of evidence.
– Why it matters: This is the clearest escalation point for early discovery.

RICO (18 U.S.C. §1962)
– Enterprise = sockpuppet network. Predicate acts = fraud + identity theft + obstruction. Pattern = ongoing since March 2025.
– Why it matters: RICO upgrades this from harassment to organized crime.

International Vectors

EUROPOL EC3: Fraud and credential laundering across EU contexts.

Why it matters: These vectors prove global scope, defeating minimization (“just a troll”).

Registrar Privacy Sidebar (Porkbun/AWS/NC)

If real info given to Porkbun: discovery subpoenas unlock true identity.

If fake info given: that’s fraud itself under OR/NC statutes.

AWS involvement: billing or hosting logs tie back to WA state, bringing in their false credential statute.

Why it matters: Either outcome strengthens the case. Privacy shielding is not protection but evidence of intent.

Training Insight

For PIs and legal trainees:

1. Jurisdiction multiplies vectors. Each state or company touched by fraud becomes a venue.

2. Privacy services are double-edged swords: they hide from the public but expose to subpoenas.

3. The turning point is always obstruction — once a harasser tampers with a legal notice, courts treat discovery as urgent.
