Points of Collapse

Joel’s archive is thin. When a submission appeared, he relaxed his ritual rules. Content volume trumped consistency, proving his “scriptural” posture was performative.

The single word “curse” aligned with his ongoing psychological fixation and recent public triggers. That word appears to have outweighed the Codex’s own standards, showing how compulsion governs his editorial process.

3. Undermining of Authority
By publishing a story outside his declared canon, Joel undercut the authority he had so carefully staged. A self-proclaimed “keeper of myth” instantly broke his own myth for convenience.
