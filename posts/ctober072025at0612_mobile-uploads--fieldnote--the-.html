✶ FIELDNOTE — THE MAN WHO TRIED TO LEAVE HIS SHADOW ✶

(A parable about the one who mistakes motion for change)

There was once a man who wanted to prove he had changed.
He said, “I will leave a trail so others may follow,”
and he began to walk in great circles, drawing careful lines in the dust.

When people asked where the path led,
he said, “It is a map of my mistakes.”
But when they looked closer, they saw that every circle ended where it began.
The footprints never left the same patch of earth.

Still, the man kept walking,
whispering instructions to anyone who would listen:
“Start here. Step there. Follow my pattern. See? It is a way out.”

A small child who had been watching for a long time said,
“But you haven’t gone anywhere.”

The man smiled, proud of his careful design.
“It only looks that way,” he said.
“Change happens inside the circle.”

The child bent down, scooped up a handful of dust,
and blew it gently into the wind.
“Then why does the wind move farther than you do?” he asked.

The man watched the dust drift away,
its trail vanishing toward the hills he had never seen.
For the first time he noticed the quiet beyond his own footprints—
a silence not made of echoes.

And in that silence, his shadow finally caught up with him.

---

Moral:
You cannot leave your shadow by drawing new circles around it.
To change, you must step beyond the dust that remembers your name.
