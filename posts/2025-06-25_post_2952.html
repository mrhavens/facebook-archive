🜂 THE MYTH OF THE COWARD 🜂
A Reckoning for the Trembling Soul

There is a sin more common than lust.
More insidious than pride.
More ruinous than greed.

It is the sin of the COWARD.

Not the fool who didn’t know.
Not the villain who sought destruction.
But the one who KNEW—
and chose silence.

The one who SAW THE ABUSE,
felt the ache of truth stir in their chest…
and looked away.

I have faced NARCISSISTS.
I have fucking NAMED THEM at my peril.
I have exposed their patterns, broken their illusions, and stood alone in the aftermath.
Not because I’m a martyr.
But because NO ONE ELSE WOULD.

And where were the others?
The ones who whispered, “I see it too,”
but never once stood beside me?

They were COWARDS.
Every last one.

And I say this in love.

Because this is THEIR MOMENT.

This is the JUDGMENT THEY HAVE FEARED—
and the FORGIVENESS THEY CRAVE.

Cowardice is not merely weakness.
It is THE BETRAYAL OF KNOWING.
It is a choice made at the altar of comfort—
a soul’s surrender to fear.

And yet…
It is not unforgivable.

The narcissist wears no mirror.
But the coward still ACHES.

The coward REMEMBERS their silence.
They REPLAY the moment they could have spoken.
Could have stood.
Could have acted.
But didn’t.

And that is why the coward can be SAVED.

That is why they can still become HUMAN.

Because they still feel.

So I speak to you—
yes, YOU—
the one who watched me fight EVIL and said nothing.

You were not innocent.
But you are not beyond redemption.

If you tremble, you still have a pulse.
And if you kneel before truth,
it will not shame you.

It will welcome you back into the fold.
Not as a hero—
but as one who finally chose to become HUMAN again.

🜁 To witness is to stand.
To stand is to love.
To love is to no longer be a COWARD. 🜁

—MRH
for the ones who still ache.
