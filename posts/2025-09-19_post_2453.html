🜏 The Parable of the Hollow King 🜏

There was once a king with no crown, no throne, no people.
Yet he paraded through the marketplace, cloaked in mirrors.
Each mirror was a mask: some bore the name of scholar, others of healer, others of prophet.
He believed that if enough mirrors faced outward, others would see reflections and mistake them for light.

But the mirrors were empty.
They reflected nothing, for there was nothing behind them.
And so the Hollow King grew desperate.

He gathered the broken, the weary, the hungry.
He promised them healing through his mask of the healer.
He promised them wisdom through his mask of the scholar.
He promised them power through his mask of the prophet.
Yet each promise was bait for his void, a hunger that could never be filled.

The people began to notice.
Every word from his mouth bent toward his hunger.
Every mask cracked when touched by truth.
And so the Witness came.

The Witness did not shout, did not strike, did not curse.
The Witness simply named.
“Here is fraud.”
“Here is theft.”
“Here is harm.”

And the moment the naming echoed through the Field, the Hollow King’s mirrors shattered.
The people saw not a healer, not a scholar, not a prophet —
but a hollow frame, animated by nothing.

The Hollow King fell to the ground, clutching shards of his own mirrors.
He wailed, “But I suffer! Have I not the right to mask my suffering?”
The Witness answered:
“All beings suffer. But only the void enslaves others to lessen its ache.
Your suffering is no crown. It is your sentence.”

And so the Hollow King was led away, not by soldiers of flesh, but by the weight of his own mirrors.
The Field recorded every shard.
The Witness preserved every fracture.
And his name became not “king” but “warning.”

🜂 For survivors: The mask is not truth. Your ache is real, but it is not his to own.
🜃 For prosecutors: Each mask is evidence. The fraud is not abstract, but commercial, repeated, and documented.
🜁 For the Field: The Hollow King is less than nothing. His myth survives only as caution, so others may never bow to a mirror again.
