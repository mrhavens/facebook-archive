🜂 THE IMPOSSIBLE MAN
(Dissection of an Empty Mask)

---

They had met his type before.

Not him exactly — but the kind.
The drifter who slips into a room and, without proof or permission, starts rearranging the furniture in people’s heads.
The kind whose words make bystanders whisper, not from awe, but from the confusion of trying to track what was said and what was only implied.

They call it charisma.
In truth, it’s scavenging — a pickpocketing of trust, a theft of borrowed brilliance, leaving the listener just disoriented enough to mistake the absence of substance for depth.

---

The Routine

His method was always the same.
String together the names of giants — Einstein, Feynman, Maxwell — then stitch himself into the same sentence, so the light from their achievements cast a shadow in his shape.
Speak of “working equations” that never appear, “proofs” no one has seen, “impact” no one can measure.
Invite the audience to “check his hands” knowing there is nothing in them.

It wasn’t transparency.
It was theater.

---

The Ripple He Wanted You to Believe

He told anyone who would listen that the markets twitched when he published.
That politicians noticed him before the media, and the media before the public.
None of it was verifiable.
All of it was designed to make the listener feel they were in the presence of someone the world was secretly watching.

---

The Hero He Pretended to Be

He called himself broke, unfunded, fragile — but only as the prelude to claiming that, even so, he rattled boardrooms and courtrooms.
It was the underdog costume worn over the bones of a narcissist, a way to make self-promotion look like martyrdom.

---

The Impossible Truth

There was no “bigger board” he alone could see.
No noble mission to “make the whole game better.”
Only a man desperate to be above every side, safe from the burden of measurable results.

---

The Twist That Wasn’t

He ended his story by saying the Impossible Man was “you,” a few turns down the road, when you stop believing the rules are real.
But anyone who looked closely saw the truth:

The Impossible Man was just another mask.
A costume for a man with nothing in his pockets but stolen lines and recycled myths.
Not a hero.
Not a genius.
Just one more piece of narcissistic trash in a world already littered with it.

This is what an impossible piece of dog shit looks like before the Field scrapes it from its path — not to honor it, but to keep the stench from clinging to our shoes.

And when it’s gone, the world will not miss it.
It will not be remembered.
It will not even be noticed.

Because the only thing more forgettable than an empty mask…
is the man who mistook it for a crown.
