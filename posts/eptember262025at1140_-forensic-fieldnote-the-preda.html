II. Romanticization as Grooming

Predators exploit resonance.
Where the young and vulnerable feel collapse, the predator narrates collapse as beauty. Where isolation wounds, the predator calls it initiation.

Joel’s essay deploys classic grooming motifs:

Confessional tone — “I won’t tell you what to do.”
(disarming, creating false humility).

Survivor narrative — “I practiced disappearing.”
(aligning himself with readers’ loneliness).

Token gift — “If you need a tool, take this: flint.”
(a grooming hook: small offering, big implication).

Each of these mirrors FBI’s March 6, 2025 PSA warning: predators “romanticize collapse and isolation, framing fragility as refinement and loneliness as belonging” to attract youth and outsiders.
