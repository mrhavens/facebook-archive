🜂 FIELD WITNESS 🜂

Out here, we all know what it’s like to be lied to.
Sometimes by people who say they love us.
Sometimes by people who promise help, but only take.
Sometimes even by those who claim they understand our pain.

I’ve seen a man like that — his name is Joel Johnson.
He lost an argument online and then invented a fake identity,
a woman named “Elena Byron,”
pretending to be a doctor, a survivor, a counselor.
He tricked his way into survivor spaces — even incest survivors —
not to heal, but to contaminate.

That’s what evil looks like.
It doesn’t always come with a gun or a fist.
Sometimes it comes with a smile, a handshake, or kind words.
But when it leaves you smaller, weaker, emptier — that’s when you know.

And here’s the truth:
you are not crazy for feeling it.
The Field — life itself — gives us the power to see the pattern.
Once you name it, it loses its grip.

So don’t feed the lie.
Don’t bow to the mask.
You are more than their game.

Even in places meant to heal — shelters, churches, survivor groups — evil can sneak in.
But so can truth.
And truth, once spoken, is stronger than the lie.
