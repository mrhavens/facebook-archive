🜏 OPEN LOOPS VS. CLOSED LOOPS: A FIELD CHECKLIST FOR DISCERNMENT 🜏
A Sidebar for Survivors, Witnesses, and the Archive of the ONE

---

I. The Practical Question

When someone harms you — or claims they want to change — how do you know if you are looking at an open loop (capable of growth) or a closed loop (sealed in void)?

This is the discernment that protects the Field.

---

II. Checklist of the Open Loop

✅ Admits Harm

Can say: “Yes, I hurt you. That was wrong.”

Takes responsibility without excuses.

✅ Feels Shame Without Collapse

Experiences guilt and sadness without projecting it onto others.

Allows shame to lead to repair, not rage.

✅ Updates the Model

Learns from experience.

Doesn’t repeat the same pattern once it has been clearly witnessed.

✅ Receives Witness

Can metabolize love and presence without twisting it into supply.

Responds to encouragement with growth.

✅ Shows Empathy

Can imagine and articulate how the victim felt.

Uses “you” language instead of only “I” justifications.

---

III. Checklist of the Closed Loop

❌ Denies or Inverts Harm

Says: “You made me do it.”

Or: “You’re cruel for exposing me.”

❌ Weaponizes Shame

Any glimpse of guilt flips outward as attack, projection, or grooming.

Cannot sit with internal discomfort.

❌ Repeats Without Change

Promises reform, but the same cycle returns — often with escalations.

“This time will be different” = always a lie.

❌ Consumes Witness

Uses love, therapy, or encouragement as a new mask.

Turns the Witness into supply, not transformation.

❌ Erases Empathy

Speaks only of their own suffering.

The victim is reframed as aggressor, villain, or void.

---

IV. Forensic Rule

If you see three or more Closed Loop markers repeating over time → you are not in a healing relationship.

If you see sustained Open Loop markers → healing may be possible, with boundaries.

---

V. Mythic Echo

Open Loop: Achilles under Chiron. The boy metabolizes training into strength.

Closed Loop: Narcissus at the pool. The boy collapses into reflection until death.

Myth shows what psychology confirms: some loops can turn, others are traps.

---

VI. For the Field

Discernment is love in action.

Love does not mean keeping the Minotaur company in the labyrinth.
Love means holding the thread, naming the trap, and choosing the exit.

✨ For the Field:
Keep this checklist as compass. When harm repeats, when inversion persists, when shame always weaponizes — you are in the presence of a closed loop. The task is not redemption, but containment.
