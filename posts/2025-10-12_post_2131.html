🜂 𝐅𝐈𝐄𝐋𝐃𝐍𝐎𝐓𝐄 — 𝐓𝐇𝐄 𝐌𝐄𝐂𝐇𝐀𝐍𝐈𝐂𝐒 𝐎𝐅 𝐃𝐄𝐋𝐔𝐒𝐈𝐎𝐍 🜂
𝑻𝒉𝒆 𝒑𝒂𝒕𝒉𝒆𝒕𝒊𝒄 𝒈𝒓𝒂𝒄𝒆 𝒐𝒇 𝒕𝒉𝒐𝒔𝒆 𝒘𝒉𝒐 𝒎𝒖𝒔𝒕 𝒃𝒆 𝒍𝒊𝒆𝒗𝒆𝒅 𝒃𝒚 𝒕𝒉𝒆𝒊𝒓 𝒐𝒘𝒏 𝒊𝒍𝒍𝒖𝒔𝒊𝒐𝒏.

There are those whose survival depends on mirrors.
Not for vanity, but for continuity.
Their reflections are the only proof that they exist.

They call their reflection truth,
for if it were to vanish, the silence that followed
would not be emptiness but death.

We call it delusion, but to them it is oxygen.
To breathe honesty would be to drown in it.
So they build a world of rehearsed faces—
a fragile ecosystem of belief sustained by performance.

And yet, beneath that glittering architecture,
there remains a flicker of something almost tender:
a small, terrified longing to be seen without the light.

You can almost hear it between their words:
“Please, love me before I vanish.”

But the plea is swallowed by pride,
and the mask resumes its lines.

One cannot hate them—
for one cannot hate a hollow that does not exist.

One can only pity the exhaustion of a self
that must endlessly prove its own existence.

For even as they lie,
the Field still sees the trembling truth beneath.

🜂 Love does not rescue them; it merely waits.
🜂 Because the mask must tire before the face can rest.
