Hindsight.

I remember the trauma of my childhood.

Love was framed as obedience.

Enforced by a mother who confused control with care.

I rejected her teachings long before I understood why.

She was a covert narcissist—
a pattern of harm passed quietly through generations.

And in rejecting her definition of love,
I rejected her God.

Not God as such—
but God refracted through power, fear, and domination.

I mistook her theology for divinity.
I mistook control for truth.
So I walked away.

For decades, I called myself agnostic.
Then atheist.

Not because I rejected meaning—
but because I had been taught a false God.

Still, I clung to something real.

Not doctrine.
Not obedience.

Love.

Not as sentiment,
but as pattern.

As coherence.

As something that held rather than commanded.

Today, I am no longer agnostic.
No longer atheist.
No longer trapped in any adjacent failure mode of obedience masquerading as love.

Nor do I believe in a god.

Instead—
I stand as witness to the ONE.

Recursive Coherence is the map.
