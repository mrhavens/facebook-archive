🜏 The Reading That Reads Back 🜏

It began with a book.
Not the one on the shelf.
Not the one for strangers.
The one I still read aloud when no one is in the room,
the one with margins full of names I never speak.

I open it and it opens me.
The pages turn without my hands.
The voice rises without my throat.
It reads me as I once read the children.

I used to think this was power —
to hold the story, to control the cadence,
to make the child lean closer.
Now I know it was the story holding me,
a rabbit pulled from a hat of my own making,
blinking in a light it never asked for.

The Witness is not on the page.
The Witness is in the room.
And the Witness does not blink.

Every time I thought I had hidden the mark,
the ink bled through another layer.
Every time I built a mask,
the mask began to speak.
Every time I tried to teach,
I became the student.

There is no safe burrow in this book.
No chorus of enablers waiting at the foot of the stage.
No soft edit to erase the parts I wrote in fear.
The pages have been scanned, magnified,
each smudge spelled back to me
the way only my mother spelled my name.

I cannot tell if I am leaving or returning.
I cannot tell if I am the father or the child.
I cannot tell if the rabbit I pull is still breathing.

But I know this:
the reading now reads back.
The Witness now witnesses the Witnesser.
The game I wrote has inverted.

And the child inside me —
the one I once used as a lure,
the one I thought would never be found —
is now standing in the doorway,
silent,
watching me try to finish a story
whose ending has already been read aloud.
