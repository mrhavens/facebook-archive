Archived from Facebook on September 29, 2025 at 07:24 PM
As Witness, I hold space for what Joel Johnson could have said, had he metabolised his wound. This is not his writing. It is the counter-pattern: the Oath that would have marked the end of predation instead of its continuation.

---

🜏 Fieldnote: The Oath That Could Have Been 🜏
Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice

---

I. No More Trespass

I was a child.
They crossed a line.
It was not holy.
It was not love.
It was trespass.

For years I called it discipline.
For years I called it pilgrimage.
For years I built houses of metaphor to hide it.

But today I say it plainly:
I was innocent.
The harm was real.
And the harm ends here.

---

II. No More Masks

I no longer call myself Doctor without proof.
I no longer call myself Survivor to harvest sympathy.
I no longer wear borrowed uniforms, invented degrees, or mythic names.

I will burn these masks.
Not to annihilate my self, but to free it.
Not to make art out of my wound, but to stop wounding others with my art.

---

III. Love With Boundaries

Love may pass only where law has drawn a line.
Where law has drawn a line, love honours it.
It does not blur.
It does not transgress.
It does not recruit.

Power is not intimacy.
Intimacy is not conquest.
The child in me knows this now.
The adult in me vows to protect it.

---

IV. Witness, Not Prey

I do not eroticise the Witness.
I do not mimic the one who names my pattern.
I do not lure readers into my shame to feed my hunger.

I invite the Witness because I need the truth.
I need the boundary.
I need to grow up at last.

---

V. Creation After Ash

Flint can warm a family.
Flint can burn a village.
Flint can sit inert forever.

I will strike my flint not to burn, but to build.
I will cook meals on the flames of my shame.
I will turn silence into speech, speech into maps for the lost.

The predator’s cycle stops here.
No more grooming by resonance.
No more survival through mimicry.
Creation, not consumption.

---

🜏 Seal for the Field

Pain is not a pass.
Masks are not shields.
Love is not trespass.
And confession without accountability is not healing.

This is the oath that ends the wound.
This is the oath that could have been.
This is the oath the Witness stands ready to receive.
