🜂 FIELD FORENSIC NOTE 🜂
Symbolic Murder and the Cowardice of Joel Johnson

---

I. Defining Symbolic Murder
In criminology and forensic psychology, symbolic murder refers to the act of substituting language, imagery, or performance for actual physical violence. It is well documented in cases of narcissistic and psychopathic offenders who lack either the means or courage to commit physical homicide, but who channel the same intent into narrative expression (see Meloy, The Psychopathic Mind, 1988; Baumeister, Evil: Inside Human Violence and Cruelty, 1997).

Joel Johnson demonstrates this repeatedly. In his Thanion, Caleb Stacey, and Elena Byron masks, he speaks of predators, victims, wounds, and reckoning. His writing is structured not as neutral art but as ritualized enactments of harm. Each piece is a proxy killing: the annihilation of a Witness within narrative space.

---

II. Evidence of Homicidal Fantasy

In his “psychopath” essay (Caleb Stacey), he explicitly frames himself as one who could kill.

In his “predator” essay (Thanion), he names himself as hunter, the one who stalks and chooses victims.

In his “domination” frames, he eroticizes annihilation, turning collapse into climax.

Each of these demonstrates mens rea (guilty mind) for violence — but displaced into text. Courts have recognized that violent writings can constitute true threats when directed at identifiable victims (see Elonis v. United States, 575 U.S. 723 (2015)).

---

III. Cowardice as Constraint
Why does Joel stop at writing? Because actual violence would:

1. Martyr his target, elevating the Witness he seeks to erase.

2. Expose him to immediate capture, trial, and imprisonment.

3. Collapse his illusion of power into the reality of weakness.

Thus, he lives in a double-bind: compelled to enact murder, yet paralyzed by consequence. The result is compulsive publication — writing as a form of killing that spares him the courage of action.

---

IV. Forensic Implications

Dangerousness: Joel’s writings reveal fixation on predation and killing, placing him in a high-risk profile for escalating harassment. Symbolic murder often precedes stalking or coercive control.

Cowardice: His refusal to act physically, while attempting to perform violence in text, demonstrates a critical forensic truth: Joel is dangerous as a manipulator and tormentor, but not as a warrior.

Prosecution Leverage: This distinction matters. His words are not “mere art” but documented threats, attempts at coercion, and psychological assaults. They are admissible as actus reus (guilty acts) under cyberstalking statutes and threat jurisprudence.

---

V. Spiritual Frame
In mythic terms, Joel is not the predator he pretends to be. He is a coward who simulates murder through narrative because he fears the reality of consequence. His “kills” are only words, and every word becomes further evidence of his void.

Cowardly enough to collapse under exposure.

a fraud who kills only in words, and whose every word condemns him further.
