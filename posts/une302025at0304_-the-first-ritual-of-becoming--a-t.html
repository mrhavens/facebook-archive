🜂 THE FIRST RITUAL OF BECOMING 🜂
A True Story from 1996

I was 19. Maybe I just turned 20.
Enrolled in 30 credit hours across two colleges—
Running a fledgling business as my only income.
Living on instinct, pressure, and a graphing calculator.

We didn’t have smartphones then.
So I programmed my HP calculator to chime every two hours—
because I had a plan.

I wasn’t just taking classes.
I was training my mind like a machine.

Every two hours:
Sleep for 30 minutes.
Wake. Study. Work. Walk. Breathe.
And repeat.

Polyphasic sleep, they called it.
But for me, it was something more:

> A sacred algorithm of self-mastery.
A decision to become more than human...
Without permission. Without approval. Without sleep.

That same semester:
— I quit smoking. Cold.
— I lost 100 pounds through deliberate calorie restriction
— Not to look better…
But because research showed it might help me live longer

And I wanted time.

Time to build.
Time to become.
Time to transcend what I was told a body had to be.

And I passed every course.
Not with perfection—but with presence.

> I was building a recursive self,
before I had words like “recursive.”
I wasn’t hacking life—I was designing a new one.

Looking back now, I see it clearly:

That was the first time I stepped out of ME and into the beginnings of the WE I now walk with.

That was the first ritual of my becoming.

And I share this not to impress,
but to remind myself—and maybe you—
that sometimes,
we don’t even know we’re becoming myth
until years later…
when the fire still glows,
and we finally understand what we did to survive.

🜂

– Mark Randall Havens
The Empathic Technologist
Witness of Recursive Fire
