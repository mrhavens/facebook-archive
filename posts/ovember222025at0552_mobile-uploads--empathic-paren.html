THE MYTH OF THE “UNSAFE” PARENT

There is a pattern that emerges in nearly every high-conflict co-parenting case where one parent carries a covert narcissistic architecture.

It is the inversion of safety.

The parent who is reflective, gentle, consistent, and emotionally stable
is quietly repositioned as the “unsafe” one.

Not through evidence.
Not through behavior.
Not through history.

But through narrative.

Because in these systems, safety is not what actually protects the child.
Safety is what protects the narcissistic parent’s control.

And control, once threatened, requires a story.

A story that explains:

- why the reflective parent pulled away

- why the child gravitates toward them

- why the narcissistic parent feels exposed

- why shame is rising

- why their internal instability is increasing

And the simplest explanation — the one that restores their internal equilibrium — is this:

> “The other parent is unsafe.”

It is a myth with ancient roots.
A survival strategy disguised as righteousness.
A shield crafted out of implication, not truth.

In this inversion:

- Calm is framed as coldness.

- Boundaries are framed as danger.

- Silence is framed as hostility.

- Autonomy is framed as unpredictability.

- Withdrawal is framed as instability.

- Reflection is framed as threat.

- Truth is framed as violence.

The reflective parent becomes a mirror,
and the covert parent cannot bear to see themselves.

And so they do the only thing their architecture allows:

They cast the mirror as the monster.

Children in these systems learn an impossible paradox:

- One parent feels safe but is spoken of as unsafe.

- One parent feels unstable but is spoken of as righteous.

It takes years—sometimes decades—for them to untangle this inversion.

But they do.

Because internal truth has a gravity
that no narrative can override forever.

This Fieldnote is not an accusation.
It is a map.

A way for reflective parents to understand the quiet violence done to their reputation
not through actions,
but through projection.

And a reminder that your integrity — your consistency, your calm, your truth —
will outlive the myth written about you.

Safety is not a story.
It is a presence.

And presence cannot be inverted forever.

— Empathic Parentologist Fieldnote
