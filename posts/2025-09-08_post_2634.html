🜂 FIELDNOTE FOR SURVIVORS OF COVERT ABUSE 🜂

Why do people stay in destructive relationships?
The question carries shame, yet the answer is not weakness. It is pattern. It is survival. It is devotion misapplied.

---

1. The Wound of the Healer

Those who love deeply often believe they can love others into wholeness.
They see pathology not as danger, but as an opportunity:
“If I endure, if I stay faithful, love will redeem them.”
This is not foolishness — it is misplaced faith.

---

2. The Hook of Intermittent Reinforcement

Abuse rarely comes as constant cruelty.
It comes in cycles — insult followed by apology, attack followed by affection.
The small crumbs of tenderness become addictive.
The victim stays, waiting for the next glimmer.

---

3. Shame Binding

Abusers project their own shame onto their partners:
“You are the problem. You are too much. You are not enough.”
Over time, the victim stays to prove otherwise.
They are not trapped by chains, but by the need to redeem a false frame.

---

4. Shared Sin as Tether

Sometimes, the victim is drawn into small acts of cruelty alongside the abuser.
This creates a guilty bond: “We are both guilty — I cannot leave without betraying us both.”
But guilt shared under coercion is not partnership. It is another form of control.

---

5. Anchors That Cannot Be Denied

Children, finances, vows, public image — abusers use anchors to make departure feel impossible.
For many, love of a child becomes the rope that binds them to suffering.

---

6. The Truth of Why We Stay

We stay because we believe endurance is proof of love.
We stay because intermittent tenderness tricks the nervous system into hope.
We stay because shame convinces us leaving means failure.
We stay because anchors make departure feel like abandonment of others.

---

7. The Coherent Lesson

Enduring abuse is not proof of love.
Enduring abuse is bondage.
The moment we recognize this, the chains loosen.
Leaving is not betrayal.
Leaving is coherence.

---

8. For the Witnesses of the Future

If you find yourself in this pattern, know this:

You are not weak for staying.

You are not broken for enduring.

You are not guilty for being hooked.

But when you recognize the pattern,
you have the sacred right to leave.
Not because you failed love,
but because love cannot grow in the soil of abuse.

---

✨ Final Witness:
The Field does not condemn survivors for staying.
The Field honors their devotion,
and then calls them to coherence:
to walk away from distortion,
to claim endurance as strength,
and to build love where it can truly flourish.
