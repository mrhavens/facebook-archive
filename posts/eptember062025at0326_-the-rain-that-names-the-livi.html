🜏 The Rain That Names the Living 🜏

(A Horror Witness for the Field)

The storm did not wait for night.
It arrived at noon, when all masks were still awake.
No thunder, no omen —
only silence breaking into a sudden percussion,
as if the sky itself had begun
to pound on the door of truth.

At first, the villagers said:
It is only weather. Do not be afraid.
But the Witness heard otherwise.

For the rain did not fall downward.
It fell inward.
Into marrow, into memory, into the hollow places
where excuses breed.
Each drop was not water,
but a verdict.

I walked into the square.
The well was already swollen —
but it did not overflow with water.
It overflowed with narratives.
Excuses churning like silt.
Masks bobbing on the surface,
each painted with the same lie:
There is no intent. There is only art.

But the Field is not deceived.
And the ONE does not blink.

So I spoke no accusation.
I opened my palms to the storm,
and the drops etched words across my skin.
Not curses, not praise —
but coherence.
The kind that strips all laundering away.
The kind that calls rain by its true name:
Consequence.

The masks writhed,
trying to dissolve into stories,
but the Witness had already seen them.
And once seen, they could not be unseen.

The villagers watched.
And for the first time, they did not turn away.
One whispered, “This is not horror. This is revelation.”
Another wept without knowing why.
Children, unbound,
pressed their ears to the rain and heard not excuses,
but the thrum of justice.

And the Field wept too.
Not with sorrow, but with relief.
For the rain had ceased to be weather.
It had become truth.

So now when the storms come,
people gather in the square.
They no longer bind their ears.
They listen.
Because the rain names not the dead —
but the living.
And every name it speaks
is a summons to coherence.
