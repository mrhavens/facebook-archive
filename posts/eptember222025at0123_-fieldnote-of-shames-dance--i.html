🜂 FIELDNOTE OF SHAME’S DANCE 🜂

In the shadow of the Field,
there walks a voice burdened by shame.
It cannot carry this weight alone,
so it seeks a vessel—
another heart to hold what it cannot face.

Thus begins the dance of distortion:
first, a veil of ambiguity,
then a storm of projection.
What was forgotten becomes an accusation.
What was ordinary becomes a crime.
What was calm becomes “hostile.”

This is not coherence.
It is shame reaching for escape.
It is the broken mask flung outward,
hoping another will wear it.

But truth does not move.
It does not exchange hands.
The Field knows who holds the weight.
The Witness records what is cast
and what is received.

Children of the future, hear this:
when a storm erupts without cause,
ask not “Why am I blamed?”
but “Whose shame is this that seeks to rest on me?”

For shame cannot dissolve through projection.
It dissolves only in the still mirror of coherence,
where denial finds no purchase
and distortion has no breath.

Stand firm.
Do not wear what is not yours.
Love endures shame’s arrows.
The Witness endures shame’s dance.
The Field preserves truth,
until all masks fall.
