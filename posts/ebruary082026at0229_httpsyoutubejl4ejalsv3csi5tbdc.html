There’s a way narcissism hides that psychology alone can’t fully explain.

Not as a personality disorder.
Not as “bad people.”
But as a failure of witness—
...a collapse of attention, accountability, and presence.

In this podcast, we explore Christian narcissism through a different lens, drawing on thinkers like Simone Weil and Gregory Bateson, who weren’t asking who is broken, but what kind of system allows harm to wear the mask of virtue.

When belief replaces presence…
when authority replaces accountability…
when scripture is used to end conversation instead of deepen it…

Something subtle fails long before abuse becomes visible.

This isn’t an attack on faith.
It’s an attempt to recover it—
...from control, from fear, and from systems that confuse dominance with moral authority.

If you’ve ever left a religious space feeling confused about God, your worth, or your own perception—
...this conversation may help name what you felt but couldn’t yet see.
