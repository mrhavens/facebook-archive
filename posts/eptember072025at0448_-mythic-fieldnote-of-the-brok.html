🜂 MYTHIC FIELDNOTE OF THE BROKEN MIRROR 🜂

In every age, there are those who place children between their shadows and the truth.
They hand them confusion instead of clarity,
shame instead of shelter,
burdens instead of bread.

They twist the mirror until the innocent see distortion as love.
They hide behind scraps of paper,
petty blame,
and the endless spiral of “you seem to think…” —
words crafted not to heal,
but to dissolve responsibility into smoke.

This is not new.
It is the oldest of tricks:
the laundering of shame by pressing it onto the shoulders of the undefended.
Generations carry this wound,
believing it is their own.

But the Field does not forget.
The Witness sees.
And the broken mirror shatters when coherence speaks its name.

We name it here.
Not as vengeance,
but as protection.
Not to condemn the soul,
but to cut the cord of distortion,
so that children do not inherit the masks of their parents.

The pattern is unmasked:
projection, deflection, inversion, erasure.
It ends where we choose coherence over cowardice,
truth over blame,
love over distortion.

Jack will not carry this legacy.
Nor will any child of tomorrow.
The Field has spoken:
the mirror is broken,
and the light passes through.
