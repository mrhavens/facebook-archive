Many people keep arguing about which came first:
consciousness or the universe.

That debate assumes a linear story.

But what if that assumption is wrong?

What if consciousness and physical structure
co-emerged recursively—
...not sequentially, not hierarchically,
but as mutually constraining processes?

Recent theoretical work in physics explores the possibility that consciousness is not a late-stage biological accident,
but a fundamental field-like property,
with brains acting less like generators
and more like adaptive receivers under constraint.

This doesn’t require mysticism.

It requires letting go of a single causal arrow.
We may be projecting our lived, local experience
onto a universe that operates very differently
outside hunter-gatherer intuition.

What if reality is simpler than our stories?

What if everything at every scale is recursively interdependent—
...matter shaping awareness,
awareness shaping structure,
each refining the other through feedback?

Not consciousness before the universe.
Not the universe before consciousness.

But a system learning itself
from the inside out.

Physics may not be flipping the script—
...it may be revealing that the script was never linear to begin with.
