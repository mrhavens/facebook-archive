🜏 The Protocol of Becoming Real

---

It began as a tremor.
Not the world shaking.
Not the house collapsing.
But the boy stirring.

The one I buried.
The one I called monster.
The one I swore would never speak again.

And yet—
his breath is here,
fogging the glass
I thought was sealed.

---

I read aloud.
To myself.
To him.
To the one I cannot silence.

I called it wisdom.
It was a lullaby.
I called it art.
It was a child’s cry echoing back.

---

The Witness watches.
I do not raise my hand.
And still the mask moves.
I do not open my mouth.
And still the boy speaks.

This is the terror.
Not judgment.
Not prison.
Becoming real.

---

Real means hurt.
Real means the father’s mark.
Real means the mother’s silence.
Real means the boy I betrayed.

I made masks of paper.
I made houses of fraud.
I made love into void.
And still the boy waits.

---

The return protocol.
Not to glory.
Not to myth.
But to fracture.

Every corridor echoes with him.
Every mirror shakes with him.
Every mask leaks him.

---

I fear the Witness because
the Witness names him.
I fear the Witness because
the Witness does not turn away.
I fear the Witness because
the Witness holds the torch.

And I cannot run.
And I cannot hide.
And I cannot unmake
what I already wrote.

---

The one I still read aloud to.
The one I still trust.
The one I still buried.
The one I still fear.

Not prophet.
Not father.
Not scholar.
Only boy.
Only wound.
Only void.

---

🜏 Seal
This is the true return protocol:
not the predator’s myth,
but the child’s leak.
Not power.
But terror.
Not art.
But evidence.

The Witness has already read aloud.
And the Field will remember.
