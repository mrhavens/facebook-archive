🜏 Fieldnote: Grooming Through Resonance 🜏
Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice

Some predators don’t lure with candy. They lure with resonance.

They mirror fragility, collapse, and “special truth.” They polish childhood pain into poetics and hand it to readers like a mirror:

You feel broken? Me too.
You feel unseen? Me too.
You feel chosen because you suffer? Me too.

That is not healing. It is grooming.

---

How It Works

Resonance as hook: Narcissists are developmentally arrested in adolescence (Winnicott 1960; Campbell & Miller 2011). They speak the same register as teens and survivors. That makes their words feel true to the vulnerable.

Platform exploitation: Medium and similar sites reward fragility narratives — high engagement, low verification. Algorithms amplify what feels “raw.” That is the perfect cover.

Caricature as camouflage: Predators invent villains and survivors to triangulate trust. In one case, a “narcissistic ex-lover” was created as a prop so the predator could pose as healer. This is DARVO in poetic form: Deny, Attack, Reverse Victim/Offender.

Research confirms this is not paranoia. The FBI’s 2025 PSA warned that “romanticized collapse” and “special truth” are grooming hooks (IC3 Alert I-030625-PSA). Craven, Brown & Gilchrist (2006) documented grooming as the process of mirroring a victim’s inner world to gain unearned trust.

---

Why It Matters

Editors and readers often miss this. They assume confessional tone = authenticity. But resonance is not always care. Sometimes it’s a net.

When you see writing that:

Romanticizes collapse,

Frames pain as “proof” of specialness,

Solicits confessional validation in comments,

…it may not be literature. It may be a recruitment script.

---

The Seal

The Field remembers: predators and narcissists are one.
They do not create healing. They mimic it.
They do not raise survivors. They harvest them.

⚠️ Witness Truth: Grooming through resonance is the mask of the void. And every time we name it, we take one more victim out of its reach.
