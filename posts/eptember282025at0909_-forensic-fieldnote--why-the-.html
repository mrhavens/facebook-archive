🜏 Forensic Fieldnote — Why the Narcissist Encodes 🜏
Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice Initiative

Narcissists live by a paradox:
They crave to be seen, yet cannot survive being known.

This is why Joel Johnson writes in riddles, encodings, and mythic ciphers:

Visibility without vulnerability.
He wants the Field to gather around his words, to admire the aesthetic, to project meaning into his fragments. Every “bruise as weather” or “absence as room” is bait: it invites sympathy without requiring accountability.

Control of interpretation.
By hiding behind ambiguity, he keeps the power to later reframe. If confronted, he can always claim: “That’s not what I meant.” Encoding is not creativity; it is an alibi generator.

Confession laundering.
Each metaphor hides a trace of truth: the strike, the bruise, the withheld apology. But by distorting it into poetics, he launders evidence into art. He admits while denying. He reveals while concealing. This is not catharsis — it is camouflage.

Recruitment by resonance.
Ambiguity allows readers — especially the wounded, the fragile, the young — to see themselves in his text. They supply their own pain to his cipher. They bond to him not because of what he said, but because of what they read into the void. This is grooming by resonance, not literature.

Fear of final exposure.
Joel cannot write plainly, because plain words would indict him. The moment he speaks directly, the mask drops. Encoding is his last defense against the Witness.

🜏 Seal for the Field
A narcissist’s cipher is not art. It is a mask.
Encoding is not depth. It is avoidance.
Ambiguity is not genius. It is fraud’s last refuge.

Joel Johnson writes not to be understood, but to stay unaccountable.
He wants to be seen, but never named.
The Witness will not allow this. Every mask he raises will be decoded. Every cipher he pens will be stripped. And the Field will see what he cannot hide:
Not brilliance, but void.
