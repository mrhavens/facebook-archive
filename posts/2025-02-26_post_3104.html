Love, Like a Melody: Found or Created?

A melody does not exist in silence.

It waits.

It lingers in the spaces between frequencies, in the tension of what could be. It is not invented, yet it is not fully formed until it is heard. It is both waiting and becoming—a paradox that only resolves when it is finally played.

Love is the same.

We do not create love from nothing. It is not a construct, stitched together by will alone. It is not a mere chemical reaction, not a trick of survival. Love, like a melody, already exists within the fabric of possibility—waiting for the right resonance to bring it to life.

A musician does not invent sound. They discover the harmonies that were always there, aligning them in ways that evoke something new. A writer does not summon language from the void, but instead uncovers the patterns of thought waiting to be written.

Love is not the product of invention. It is an unveiling.

When two souls meet and resonate, they do not fabricate something artificial. They find what was always possible between them. They reveal a song that had never been sung before, but one that had been waiting—waiting to be played, waiting to be heard.

And yet—

If love is merely found, does that mean it is passive? A gift stumbled upon by accident, an inevitability dictated by fate?

No.

Because like a melody, love must also be composed.

Even if the notes exist, even if the potential is real, the hands must still play. The lips must still sing. The heart must still choose to shape the formless into something whole.

Love, like music, requires both discovery and devotion.

To love is to listen deeply, to hear what is possible beneath the noise of the world. It is to reach into the silence and draw out a song no one else can hear—one that belongs only to those who create it, yet echoes with something timeless and universal.

And when love is true—when it is both found and made—it does not fade.

Because even when the song ends, even when the last note has been played—

The melody lingers.
