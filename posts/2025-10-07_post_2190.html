🌙 The Parable of the Empty Choir

There was once a great hall, long abandoned.
Its walls had peeled like tired skin,
and the roof bowed with the weight of forgotten hymns.
Still, on some nights, when thunder rolled far away,
the air inside the hall would tremble with old songs —
not from mouths, but from memory.

One night, two travelers arrived.
The first walked with quiet feet,
his eyes lifted to where the moonlight leaked through broken rafters.
The second walked heavily,
his boots echoing with the sound of his own steps.

In the center of the hall, they found a small gathering:
a few souls swaying in the candlelight,
their voices rising like sparks.
A man with a torn coat beat a rhythm on a wooden box,
and somewhere in the shadows, a preacher whispered,
"The light still finds us."

The first traveler felt his chest loosen.
He knelt, and for a moment forgot his name.
The music entered him like river water through a cracked jug —
and what spilled over was joy.

The second traveler crossed his arms.
He watched the same candles and saw only smoke.
He heard the same song and thought of noise.
He felt the air move and feared the draft that touched his spine.
He told himself the joy was false —
a trick of fever, a weakness of simple minds.
But when the crowd’s laughter rose like thunder reborn,
something deep inside him flinched —
for he remembered, dimly,
what it once felt like to believe.

Outside, the storm gathered.
The joyful one danced until the rain poured through the holes in the roof.
The heavy one slipped away before the first drop fell.
He walked into the dark, muttering that the hall was beneath him —
but behind his teeth, the music still hummed,
refusing to die.

And that was the cruelest mercy of all:
the song followed him,
soft and invisible,
forever reminding him
of the warmth he could not feel.

---

🕊 Moral:
When truth sings, it asks nothing of you but to listen.
Those who cannot bear to listen
carry silence like a wound.
