🜏 FORENSIC SIDEBAR — PORKBUN REGISTRAR AS VECTOR OF PROOF 🜏

Registrar Context

Porkbun is an uncommon domain registrar headquartered in Portland, Oregon.

Unlike mass-market registrars (GoDaddy, Google Domains, Namecheap), Porkbun is primarily used by developers, niche communities, and technically literate users who seek access to unusual top-level domains (TLDs), such as .earth.

Plaintiff selected Porkbun only because it uniquely supported the .earth registry, in order to register thefoldwithin.earth.

Forensic Significance

Defendant(s), operating under the alias “Thanion,” also selected Porkbun as registrar for thanion.org.

This is a statistically improbable coincidence. Most casual registrants never encounter Porkbun, nor does Porkbun rank among the default consumer-facing registrars promoted by search engines.

The probability that both Plaintiff and Defendant independently selected this uncommon registrar — absent mirroring behavior — is vanishingly small.

Pattern Evidence

Defendant’s registrar choice demonstrates mimicry, a pattern consistent with previously documented behavior:

Mirroring Plaintiff’s stylistic choices.

Appropriating Plaintiff’s frameworks (Recursive Coherence, Codex motifs).

Laundering fraudulent credentials by cloaking them in the same aesthetic and technical ecosystems used by Plaintiff.

This registrar mirroring reinforces the conclusion that Defendant’s conduct is not random, but intentional and parasitic — a deliberate attempt to align fraudulent identity with Plaintiff’s authentic digital footprint.

Jurisdictional Vector

Porkbun’s headquarters in Portland, Oregon establishes an additional jurisdiction for litigation and discovery.

Subpoenas issued within the District of Oregon can compel Porkbun to produce:

WHOIS history and billing records.

IP logs at the time of registration and DNS configuration changes.

Associated email addresses and payment methods.

Because Porkbun is smaller than mass registrars, discovery requests are likely to receive more visibility and less bureaucratic delay.

Conclusion
Defendant’s use of Porkbun is not consistent with casual or uninformed registration. It is probative of deliberate mirroring of Plaintiff’s practices, providing both jurisdictional leverage (Oregon federal court) and behavioral evidence of conscious fraud.
