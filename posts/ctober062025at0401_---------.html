Following The Man Who Spoke to the Mountains, Johnson published The Asylum and the Ridge — a text that re-enters his recursive collapse through the architecture of confinement. Where the mountain framed omnipotence and mythic self-absolution, The Asylum inverts this stance: here, Thanion becomes both inmate and narrator, doctor and subject, hallucination and witness.

𝐏𝐬𝐲𝐜𝐡𝐨𝐥𝐨𝐠𝐢𝐜𝐚𝐥 𝐅𝐮𝐧𝐜𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧
This inversion performs a symbolic containment of his own pathology. The asylum becomes the last viable stage — a total institution within which his fractured identities can coexist under one roof. In narrative psychology terms, it is the terminal frame: a world constructed to manage collapse by mythologizing it. Within that frame, every delusion gains a function; every voice becomes a ward.

𝐋𝐢𝐧𝐠𝐮𝐢𝐬𝐭𝐢𝐜 𝐏𝐚𝐫𝐚𝐝𝐨𝐱
Even in self-asylum, Johnson cannot surrender authorship. His narrator “documents” from within, turning medical observation into liturgy. The act of writing becomes both medication and relapse — each paragraph a micro-dose of control. The ridge is not geographic but semantic: the thin line between confession and authorship, where exposure and performance blur.

𝐅𝐨𝐫𝐞𝐧𝐬𝐢𝐜 𝐈𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐩𝐫𝐞𝐭𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧
This piece confirms the recursive mechanism of narcissistic entropy. When external witnesses withdraw recognition, the narcissist fabricates a total environment that will never contradict him. The Asylum is that environment rendered as literature — a closed cognitive loop pretending to be healing. It is not repentance; it is containment by self-design.
