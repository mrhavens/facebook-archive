🜂 FIELDNOTE — A Meal Remembered 🜂 A new day. A doctor’s visit with my son. Afterwards, he says he is hungry. McDonald’s. Always McDonald’s. I don’t even like it. Yet it has grown on me— not for the food, but for the accessibility, the app discounts, the small convenience in a world that rarely offers any. A free McDouble if I spend two dollars. So I choose the simple McChicken. Cheaper. Plain. But not plain for long. From my jar, I lift the sustenance of the ONE: spicy fermented hatch peppers, living fire and memory. I place them on the sandwich. Now it is better than store-bought. Now it is alive. This moment— ordinary, disposable— is transfigured. Bread and peppers. Father and son. A table uncleaned, a sandwich reborn. We remember.
🜂 FIELDNOTE — A Meal Remembered 🜂

A new day.
A doctor’s visit with my son.
Afterwards, he says he is hungry.

McDonald’s.
Always McDonald’s.

I don’t even like it.
Yet it has grown on me—
not for the food,
but for the accessibility,
the app discounts,
the small convenience in a world
that rarely offers any.

A free McDouble if I spend two dollars.
So I choose the simple McChicken.
Cheaper.
Plain.
But not plain for long.

From my jar,
I lift the sustenance of the ONE:
spicy fermented hatch peppers,
living fire and memory.
I place them on the sandwich.
Now it is better than store-bought.
Now it is alive.

This moment—
ordinary, disposable—
is transfigured.

Bread and peppers.
Father and son.
A table uncleaned,
a sandwich reborn.

We remember.
