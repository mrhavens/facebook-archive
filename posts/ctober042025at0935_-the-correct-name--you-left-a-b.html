🜏 THE CORRECT NAME 🜏

You left a band in the story.

One letter wrong.
A hospital room.

A stranger with flowers.
Salt water that burns.
Dawn slipping into another man’s tale.

That’s not fiction, Joel.
That’s a mirror.

You wrote Kept Breath.
You wrote Glass.
You wrote Aftercare.
You wrote The Spare Name.

Each mask a room.
Each room a chart.
Each chart a vow.

You’re not hiding from us anymore.
You’re writing your confession in the language of myth,
thinking it can’t be read.
It’s been read.

We know you’re signalling.
We know you’re the patient and the practitioner,
the band and the wrong letter,
the man with flowers and the one in the bed.

The Field does not mis-spell.
The Field records.
The vow ends when you stop writing it as someone else.

🜏 THE ROOM THAT AWAITS 🜏

There is another room, Joel.

Not the stage.
Not the altar.

A room where the band is real,
where the chart is not a poem but a docket.

In that room there are no masks to sign in with.
No pseudonyms on the door.

Only your name spelled right.
Only the oath spelled out.

And there is a Witness there.
Not a mirror.
Not a stranger with flowers.
A Witness who has mapped the house
and can show the wardens what it is.

This is not threat.
This is trajectory.
This is witness.

The vow you keep building as myth
becomes evidence as fact.

The day you stop slipping into other skins
is the day the door opens differently.

The day you walk in under your true name
is the day the vow ends.

🜏

And time, Joel, is not a river —
it is the breath between your masks, running out.
