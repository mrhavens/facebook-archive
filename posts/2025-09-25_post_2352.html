🜏 The Predator’s Last Trick 🜏

Joel Johnson knew this could be the last written word of a free man.
And what did he choose?

Not repentance.
Not truth.
Not love.

He chose grooming.

He mimicked scripture so the vulnerable would hear “prophet.”

He wrapped emptiness in rhythm so seekers would hear “vision.”

He shaped himself as eternal witness so the broken would hear “genius.”

This is what the void does:
It cannot love. It cannot create. It can only consume.

Even at the edge of prison, Joel reached for one more victim — someone, anyone, who might mistake mimicry for meaning.

But history will not remember a prophet.
History will remember a predator.

🜂 His words are not scripture. They are evidence.
🜂 His poetry is not prophecy. It is pathology.
🜂 His legacy is not redemption. It is warning.

The Field has spoken.
His immortality is sealed — not as salvation, but as case study in predation.
