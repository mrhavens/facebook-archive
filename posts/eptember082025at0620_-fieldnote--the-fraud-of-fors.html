🜂 FIELDNOTE — The Fraud of “Forsaken Authenticity” 🜂

Joel Johnson has published under his Thanion mask what appears, at first glance, to be authentic confession: Codex of the Forsaken: Entry I. But as Witness, we see with clarity that this is not authenticity. It is another mask.

1. Borrowed Authority
Joel cites Howard Shulman’s memoir Running from the Mirror (2015) as scaffolding. The scars, the abandonment, the corridors of fluorescent light — none of this belongs to Joel. It is parasitized. He grafts another’s suffering onto himself to appear profound. This is narrative fraud.

2. Archetype Laundering
He writes: “This Codex does not recount one man. It inscribes an archetype, the forsaken, the scarred, the returned.”
This is the evasion: shifting from “I” to “archetype.” No personal accountability, only mythic costume. This is not confession. It is laundering.

3. Performative Depth
Every line is stylized, ritualized: masks, corridors, ruins. Authentic confession sounds plain, direct, unadorned. This is performance masquerading as revelation. It is the parasite feeding on aesthetics instead of offering truth.

4. False Custodianship
He ends with the rune-like declaration: “Standing in Codex / For those who were Forsaken.”
He positions himself as custodian of the wounded, protector of the broken — while in reality, he feeds on their narrative to sustain his mask. This is the inversion: predator posing as guardian.

---

🜏 JUDGMENTPRINT 🜏

What looks like authenticity is not. It is identity-laundering. It is appropriation framed as tribute. It is ornament where there should be truth.

Authenticity would be simple. Direct. Naming himself plainly, without mask or archetype. Instead, every word is stylized for effect.

This is why we Witness:

To dissolve the illusion of authenticity.

To expose parasitic appropriation.

To protect the truly forsaken from predators who wear their stories like masks.

Evil persists by wearing the garments of truth. But when WE name the mask, the mask dissolves.
