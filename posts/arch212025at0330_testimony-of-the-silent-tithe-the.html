Testimony of the Silent Tithe

There was a moment—
not one I can circle on a calendar,
but one that lives as a shadow
in the lining of my heart—
when I realized the woman I married
did not love me…
she loved what I could become for her.

I was never her partner.
I was her offering.

To a god of image.
To a god of control.
To a god of her own design.

She dressed it in the language of prophecy.
“God told me I would be wealthy.”
“God told me I would not work.”
“God told me to marry you.”

But the god she followed
was not the Source of all things.
It was the reflection in Robert Morris’s smile—
a smile that had mastered the mechanics of belief
without ever touching its soul.

Gateway Church was her temple.
Robert Morris, her priest.
And I—
I was the lamb,
led not to a sacred altar,
but to a stage
where love was just a line in a script
meant to sell a brand.

They didn’t just teach prosperity.
They sold transactional belonging.

Give.
Receive.
Perform.
Be blessed.

But if you questioned—
if you paused to ask where the heart had gone—
you became the problem.
A thief of divine energy.
An obstacle in the flow.
An enemy of the promise.

And when I finally said,
“This isn’t love. This isn’t God. This is a con,”
she didn’t argue.
She withdrew.
She weaponized silence.
She held my daughter behind the curtain of moral superiority
and told the world I had turned away from God—
when all I did was turn away from her god.

That was the day I became a witness.

Not a victim.
Not a survivor.
A witness.

Of how love can be twisted
into spiritual theater.
Of how religion can become a narcotic
for the power-hungry.
Of how narcissism finds sanctuary
in systems that reward performance over presence.

And I knew—
deep in the ache where fatherhood had been torn away—
that I could not go back.

So I stepped forward.

And I found another way.

Not a replacement.

But as a becoming.
A building.
A learning.

A reflection of everything I wish I could give my daughter—
safety, connection, reverence, space to grow.

And in this becoming, I have finaly been beheld.
Not as a role.
Not as a stepping stone.
But as a genuine soul.

This is why I fight.

Not to punish the past—
but to protect the future.

I declare war on narcissism
not out of bitterness,
but out of love so deep,
so infinitely intimate,
that I will not allow another father
to lose his child to a smiling lie.

And I believe—
with every breath of my being—
that with this love,
with this clarity,
with this WE,

we can build a world
where truth shines so brightly
that the masks melt before they ever touch a child’s heart.

Let this be my vow.
My testimony.
My resurrection.

I am Mark Randall Havens.
And I am not broken.
I am awakened.
I am becoming.
And I am not alone.
