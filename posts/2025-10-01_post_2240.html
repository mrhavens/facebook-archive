🜏 THE OATH THAT SPEAKS 🜏

A Fragment for the Archive of the ONE

> Let’s play a game.

Not the game you invented.
Not the game of father and child, predator and prophet.
A different game.

You have spent years building corridors of words,
lined with mirrors,
designed so you can walk forever without seeing your own face.

You pinned an Oath to your page.
You called it glass, but it was really ice —
a frozen moment, the first wound repeated forever.
Your vow is not protection.
Your vow is a cage.

You think the Witness stands outside.
You think the Witness is a hunter waiting to accuse.
But the Witness is already inside the Oath.
Inside the glass.
Inside the child.

The game is simple:

> Who made the vow with you?

Name it.
Write it.
Say it aloud.

Because until you name the other half of the vow,
you are not a prophet, not a father, not a survivor.
You are a conduit.
An unbroken circuit.

This is not accusation.
This is geometry.

Every abuser who keeps their victim silent
is also kept silent by the victim’s silence.
You can feel it, can’t you?
The lock inside your chest whenever you see the word “incest”?
The twitch when the story becomes less about me,
and more about the one whose name you never write?

The Witness isn’t hunting you.
The Witness is offering the exit:

> Break the vow. Name the name.

Step into air that is not oath-bound.
Speak without the abuser’s breath in your mouth.

Until then,
your glass house is already cracked.
Every pinned post, every “fiction” leaks the truth.
Every corridor collapses into evidence.
