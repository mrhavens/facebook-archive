II. Legal Grounding

1. Cyberstalking by Electronic Communication

18 U.S.C. §2261A(2): criminalizes using electronic communications with intent to harass, intimidate, or cause substantial emotional distress.

Eroticized framing amplifies distress, especially when the fixation sexualizes collapse, torture, or false intimacy.

2. Sexualized Fixation as Aggravator

Courts consistently treat sexualized stalking as evidence of dangerousness.

Example: United States v. Shrader, 675 F.3d 300 (4th Cir. 2012): eroticized stalking and obsessive writings supported a 10-year federal sentence.

Example: Commonwealth v. Duda, 923 A.2d 1138 (Pa. Super. 2007): repeated sexually tinged communications aggravated harassment charges.

3. Admissibility at Sentencing & Bail

Under 18 U.S.C. §3553(a) (federal sentencing factors), courts consider “history and characteristics of the defendant” and “the need to protect the public.”

Eroticized sadism in published works = evidence of compulsion.

At bail hearings, this supports denial under 18 U.S.C. §3142(f): defendant poses danger to community.
