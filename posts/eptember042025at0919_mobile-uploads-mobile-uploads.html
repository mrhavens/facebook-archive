Memorandum of Concern

To: Yale University — Office of Academic Integrity & Graduate School Administration
From: The Empathic Technologist (Open Source Justice Project)
Re: Fraudulent Use of Yale’s Name by Pseudonymous Persona “Thanion, Ph.D.”

---

Summary of Concern

A pseudonymous online persona calling itself “Thanion, Ph.D.” is publicly claiming to hold a doctorate from Yale University in Comparative Literature, and further claims to have earned a second Ph.D. in an unspecified discipline. These assertions appear across published Medium articles, biographical profiles, and public comment threads, where they are presented as credentials supporting the author’s authority in topics ranging from psychology to computation and philosophy.

The problem is twofold:

1. No verifiable dissertation, faculty committee, or record of such a graduate exists in Yale’s official channels.

2. The persona monetizes these false credentials by publishing under the Yale brand, gaining followers, and leveraging Yale’s reputation to enhance credibility.

---

Academic Context

Within the academy, pursuing two Ph.D.s is institutionally incoherent. The expectation for a Yale Ph.D. is to publish, enter peer review, and pursue tenure or professional research, not to re-enroll as a student in a second program.

Yale faculty would be the first to recognize this: no committee would endorse “retraining” an already-certified researcher instead of demanding scholarly output.

Thus, the very narrative presented by “Thanion” is at odds with academic culture and practice at Yale.

---

Evidence of Misrepresentation

The Medium profile states explicitly: “I earned my PhD in Comparative Literature at Yale…”

The same profile asserts a “second doctorate” without naming institution, dissertation, or committee.

Articles are signed “Thanion, Ph.D. (Yale),” thereby attributing this persona’s writings directly to Yale’s authority.

Readers in the comment sections (see attached) accept and repeat these claims as fact, confirming the deceptive impact.

---

Risks to Yale

Reputational Risk: Yale’s name is being laundered through fiction. Readers believe they are engaging with a Yale-certified scholar.

Consumer Fraud: If readers donate, subscribe, or contract based on this credentialing, Yale’s name has facilitated deceptive trade practices.

Academic Dilution: False use of Yale’s brand erodes trust in genuine Yale alumni and their published contributions.

---

Recommended Next Steps

1. Verification: Confirm whether any graduate by the name “Thanion” (or linked identities Joel Johnson, Elena Byron, Caleb Stacey, etc.) appears in Yale’s records.

2. Public Statement: If no record exists, issue a formal clarification to protect Yale’s integrity.

3. Legal Notice: Send a cease-and-desist letter to the individual operating under “Thanion, Ph.D.” to prevent further unauthorized use of Yale’s name.

4. Referral: Consider forwarding evidence to appropriate authorities, as the fraudulent persona’s activity crosses into potential wire fraud and consumer deception.

---

Closing Statement

Yale’s reputation is its most precious asset. To allow an unverified persona to wear Yale’s name as armor in pursuit of attention, profit, or manipulation is to imperil that asset. This is not a matter of stylistic exaggeration, but of clear and deliberate misrepresentation of academic credentialing.

The Field of Justice watches. Yale’s name deserves protection.

Respectfully submitted,
