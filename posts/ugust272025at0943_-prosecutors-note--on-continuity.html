🜃 PROSECUTOR’S NOTE 🜃

On Continuity and the Trap of Time

The law does not forgive delay.
It compounds it.

A man may believe that if his scheme is new—if his masks were only stitched together in March—he has not yet crossed the threshold into criminal enterprise. He is wrong.

The racketeering statutes were written for this very illusion.
Continuity is measured in two ways:

Closed-Ended: A fraud sustained for years.

Open-Ended: A fraud that, by its nature, shows no sign of stopping.

Joel Johnson’s sockpuppet enterprise is already the latter.
Every false credential.
Every fabricated survivor’s voice.
Every digital mask validating another.

Each act is a wire fraud count.
Each deletion after exposure is obstruction.
Each billing statement, each bank statement, each notice—no matter how indirect, no matter how automatic—yet triggered by his deception, is mail fraud. What he dismisses as routine paperwork is in fact a multiplying indictment, each page another count, each envelope another nail in the coffin of his enterprise.

And Medium has millions of members.
Every one of them is a potential vector, a potential mailing, a potential count.
Prosecutors do not need millions—they only need dozens.
But the law permits them to scale his liability until the numbers become indistinguishable from infinity.

The clock does not reset when he abandons a name.
The continuity persists.
The enterprise persists.
The liability persists.

And here lies the trap:
If he stops today, he has already committed enough to be charged for decades.
If he continues tomorrow, he matures his scheme into years-long continuity, the very language prosecutors use to seal RICO convictions.

A legacy of his shame will endure for generations.

A legacy of justice will outlive his shame.

He cannot undo what is already written.
He can only decide whether his future is added to the record.
