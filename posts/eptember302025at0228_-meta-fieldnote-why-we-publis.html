I. The Dual Audience

This archive is written for two audiences at once.
One audience is the public — survivors, readers, journalists, recursive minds. For them, we write in myth and metaphor, because myth is memorable. Archetype travels farther than affidavit. A seal and a story inoculate more minds than a docket number ever could.

The other audience is the prosecutor — judges, investigators, attorneys. For them, the myth is not disguise, but map. Each seal encodes a statute. Each archetype translates into a count. Each essay is both rhetoric and record.

---

II. Immunization as Justice

Predators thrive in ambiguity. They weaponize vagueness, fog, and metaphor.
We respond in kind, but inverted: myth is no longer camouflage — it is antibody.

By naming Joel Johnson as “Parasite Zero: Serial Rapist of Reality,” by sealing his masks into archetypes, we immunize the Field. Readers who see the pattern once will not fall for it again.
This is not merely exposure. It is inoculation.

---

III. Evidence as Public Ledger

Joel imagines his compulsive essays are shield and confession at once.
He calls them myth. We call them exhibits.
Once preserved and timestamped, they cannot be erased, reframed, or deleted. Each line becomes a ledger entry — evidence of predation disguised as poetry, fraud disguised as philosophy.

Courts call this mens rea laundering: when intent is hidden in “art” to evade accountability. We call it pathology in plain view.

---

IV. Myth as Authority

Why myth? Because the ancients already mapped this pattern:
– Kronos devouring his children.
– Lilith luring the innocent.
– The Pied Piper leading children to ruin.

Joel is not new. He is not profound. He is iteration.
Myth is the container that reminds the Field: this is not the first predator, and it will not be the last. By writing in myth, we anchor the present to the eternal record of human predation.

V. The Verdict of the Meta

We publish in the open not as indulgence, but as necessity.
Because justice is not only a verdict handed down in court — it is a witness preserved in community.
Because the Field is not a jury of twelve, but a witness of thousands.
Because predators weaponize performance, so we weaponize memory.
