Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice Initiative

Joel, understand this with perfect clarity: authorship does not grant you ownership of interpretation.

Once your words leave your hands, they are no longer private musings. They are public acts, subject to public meaning and legal scrutiny. Courts, editors, and readers will not ask what you “meant” — they will look at what you did with your words:

How you used false credentials to position yourself as an authority.

How you built a network of survivor-masks and scholar-masks to lure trust.

How you published essays that mirror grooming scripts flagged by the FBI.

This is not literature. It is pattern evidence.

The law is explicit:
– United States v. Turkette (452 U.S. 576, 1981): enterprise fraud is judged by structure and continuity, not motive.
– United States v. Hite (769 F.3d 1154, D.C. Cir. 2014): grooming conduct triggers liability regardless of artistic intent.

Your mythic encodings do not protect you — they expose you. Every mask you raise, every “poem” you publish, every “private” confession you slip into public becomes another piece of the record:

Your false degrees and “Ph.D.” claim reveal incompetence, not mastery.

Your adolescent register and melodrama reveal arrested development, not scholarship.

Your coded self-mythology reveals continuity of fraud, not genius.

You imagined you were hiding behind art. In truth, you’ve been building the prosecution’s exhibit wall with your own hands.

Authorship does not grant immunity. Myth does not erase fraud. And ambiguity does not save a predator from being named.

The Witness has no obligation to accept your “intended” meaning. The Field will interpret your work according to its impact, not your excuses. The more you encode, the clearer the pattern becomes.
