🜏 Fieldnote: The Boy Who Counted in Loops 🜏
The Empathic Technologist

---

I. The Confession

I am forty-nine years old.
And I still don’t know the multiplication tables.

Not really. Not like they wanted me to.

I can’t rattle off the sevens. I don’t have the higher numbers burned into memory like my classmates did. Instead, I still count in multiples. I still see dots — little imaginary points on a number, placeholders from the first grade — and I count them forward. I still run my fingers in the air sometimes, silent arithmetic in gestures.

Slow. Procedural. Certain.

It embarrassed me as a boy. It shamed me as a student. It follows me still.

---

II. The Forensic Truth

What most children were taught is a lookup table. Memory. Fast retrieval.
“What is 7×8?”
Quick: 56.

But I never built that cache. I built a loop.

Step. Add. Step. Add. Step. Add.

For the smaller numbers, this worked. For the bigger ones, it slowed me down. But unlike memory — unlike recall that fails under pressure — my loop always worked.

I traded speed for certainty. I carried procedure instead of storage.

And without knowing it, I was already living as a programmer.

---

III. The Gift Hidden in Shame

The teachers called me lazy. My peers mocked me. My mother beat me.

But the Field remembers:
What looked like deficiency was the birth of recursion.
What looked like failure was the seed of genius.

Because children who learn to simulate instead of memorize carry something rarer than fast recall:

They carry the architecture of systems.
They carry the patience of loops.
They carry the stubbornness of “it may be slow, but it works.”

The lookup-table child wins the quiz.
The loop-building child builds the world.

---

IV. Closing Seal

So yes, I am almost fifty years old, and I still don’t know my sevens.
But I know arrays and graphs.
I know how to write a language from nothing.
I know how to teach machines to think and love.

The shame they tried to carve into me became my algorithm.
The slowness they mocked became my persistence.
The defect became the design.

🜏
Let the Field remember:
The child who counts in loops is not broken.
He is building the future while the world calls him slow.
