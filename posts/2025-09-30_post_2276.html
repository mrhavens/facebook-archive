---

I. The Mask as Supply

A narcissistic predator’s power does not come from truth.
It comes from resonance.
They build masks — survivor, healer, scholar — not to live authentically but to evoke support reflexes in others.

Because the mask looks wounded, readers feel duty.
Because the mask looks credentialed, readers assume authority.
Because the mask floats in poetic vagueness, readers fill in details with their own sympathy.

The mask itself is the supply engine:

> “See? People defend me. I am good.”

It doesn’t matter that the context is fraud.
It doesn’t matter that no specifics are given.
What matters is the appearance of a victim worth protecting.

---

II. Exposure as Cruelty

When the mask cracks, the predator cannot metabolize the shame.
Instead of accountability, they launch the cruelty flip:
– “This isn’t exposure, it’s persecution.”
– “I’m being smeared by a narcissist.”
– “They’re destroying my life.”

This is DARVO in its pure form (Freyd, 1997):
Deny, Attack, Reverse Victim and Offender.
The predator tries to turn the Witness into the villain and themselves into the martyr.

Why? Because if they can frame exposure as cruelty, they can keep supply flowing even after the fraud is documented:

> “See? I’m under attack. Rally to me.”

---

III. The Mask-Supply Loop

1. Mask — predator builds a persona (trauma survivor, psychologist, scholar).

2. Resonance — vulnerable readers empathize, fill in gaps, defend.

3. Exposure — facts surface; mask begins to crumble.

4. Cruelty Flip — predator reframes exposure as persecution, tries to harvest sympathy.

5. New Mask — predator pivots to a fresh persona, begins loop again.

This is not misfortune.
It is a deliberate survival algorithm for the void.

---

IV. The Witness’s Role

Because the Witness documents publicly, the predator’s narrative collapses.
Transparency dissolves ambiguity.
Supply dries up.
The cruelty flip fails.

The predator calls this “attack” because to them, exposure feels like annihilation.
But exposure is not cruelty.
It is accountability.
It is protection for the vulnerable.

---

V. Mythic Frame — The False Shepherd

In myth, the false shepherd wears the fleece of a lamb to draw the flock.
When the fleece is stripped away, he howls: “You are cruel! You are stealing my flock!”
But the flock was never his.
It was lured by deception.

The cruelty flip is the howl of the false shepherd as the fleece is removed.
It is not injustice.
It is the sound of truth reclaiming stolen ground.

---

VI. The Verdict of the Field

Predators call exposure “cruelty” because cruelty is the last mask left when every other mask has been removed.
They rely on masks to evoke duty and sympathy without ever providing accountability.
They rely on vagueness to leave only assumptions, resonance, and the reflex to protect a fictional neighbor.

The Field names the mechanism:

> Mask builds supply.
Exposure triggers cruelty flip.
Cruelty flip is the mask’s death rattle.

By witnessing it openly, the loop is broken.
Pain is not a pass.
Masks are not shields.
Exposure is not cruelty.

---

🔥 Seal for Posterity
The cruelty flip is not evidence of persecution.
It is evidence of a mask losing its power.
Remember this when predators howl “attack” as their fraud dissolves — it is not injustice you are hearing, but the sound of the mask collapsing under the weight of truth.
