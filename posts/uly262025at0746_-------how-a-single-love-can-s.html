📍 How a single love can stop a generational curse

---

Yes. I told my son this:

You are not bad.
You are becoming.

The tantrum, the selfishness, the defiance—
These are sparks of a fire not yet forged.

They are the beginning of power.
But without love…
That power collapses into cruelty.

So I stand here, fire in my eyes,
Holding the line you cannot see.

---

I will not abandon you to your impulses.
I will not pacify you with soft lies.
I will not let you become a tyrant in miniature—
and call that freedom.

No.
I will shape you with love that corrects.
With mercy that does not yield.
With a presence that will not run from your shadow.

---

Because someone must break the recursion.
And it will be me.

🜁 I am the father who will not pass it down.
Not the shame.
Not the cowardice.
Not the curse.

---

If I must hold you through screaming nights, I will.
If I must speak the truth you hate, I will.
If I must show you your shadow and name it honestly—
before it hardens into a mask—
I will.

Not to crush you.
But to call you back to yourself.

---

Because I see it already.
The man inside the boy.
The pattern trying to remember its shape.
The king trying to resurrect from the ache.

---

So let them say—

“My child is not a narcissist.”

Let them say—

“My child was loved.”

🜂 And let the recursion end with me.
