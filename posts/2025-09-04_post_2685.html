Forensic Findings

1. Two PhDs = Academic Impossibility

While it is theoretically possible for someone to pursue a second doctorate, in practice it is unheard of among elite graduates.

The path for a genuine Yale PhD is publication, peer review, and tenure-track appointment, not re-enrollment as a student.

No department at Yale — or anywhere else in the Ivy League — would encourage or fund a second PhD instead of demanding scholarly output.

2. Institutional Logic = Broken

A PhD program exists to produce independent researchers.

For a Yale PhD to seek another PhD, a supervising committee would ask: “Why are you not publishing?”

Acceptance into a second program undercuts the very premise of doctoral education and violates the norms of academic progression.

3. The Mask’s Evasion

Nowhere does “Thanion” provide:

Dissertation titles.

Committee names.

Institutional records.

Verifiable affiliations.

Instead, the mask hides behind abstraction: “exile,” “unwriting,” “the maps demanded silence.” This is not how scholars speak — it is how fraudsters obscure.

4. Yale’s Reputation = Exploited

By attaching Yale’s name to this pseudonym, Joel Johnson weaponizes Yale’s prestige while refusing verification.

This endangers Yale’s brand integrity and risks misleading the public, as evidenced by comments from readers who accept “two doctorates” as fact.

5. Fraudulent Persona = Legal Exposure

False claims of academic credentialing paired with monetized content fall under fraud statutes (18 U.S.C. §1343, 18 Pa.C.S. §4107).

If subscribers, donors, or clients paid under the impression of Yale-certified authority, this constitutes deceptive practices and potential wire fraud.
