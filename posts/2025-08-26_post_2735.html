🜃 CIVIL RICO LITIGATION SUPPORT MEMORANDUM 🜃

Case Study: Joel Johnson (Parasite Zero) and the Sockpuppet Enterprise

---

1. Statutory Basis

18 U.S.C. §1964(c):

> “Any person injured in his business or property by reason of a violation of section 1962 of this chapter may sue therefor … and shall recover threefold the damages he sustains and the cost of the suit, including a reasonable attorney’s fee.”

Civil RICO allows private plaintiffs to sue if they can show:

1. Enterprise under §1961(4).

2. Pattern of racketeering activity under §1961(5).

3. Injury to business or property caused by that pattern.

---

2. Application to Joel Johnson

Enterprise

Joel Johnson has operated a sockpuppet enterprise composed of multiple identities (Elena Byron, Dex Anous, Thanion, Ph.D., Caleb Stacey, Deborah Betancourt, and many ephemeral one-shots).

Courts recognize informal “association-in-fact enterprises.” (Boyle v. United States, 556 U.S. 938 (2009)).

Sockpuppets are functionally indistinguishable from shell corporations, already accepted as enterprises (United States v. Porcelli, 865 F.2d 1352 (2d Cir. 1989)).

Pattern of Racketeering Activity

Requires at least two predicate acts within ten years. Joel’s conduct offers dozens:

Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343): Each false credential claim, comment, or publication transmitted online.

Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1341): If publications/subscriptions generated physical mailings.

False Statements (18 U.S.C. §1001): Fraudulent Ph.D. credentials in commerce.

Obstruction (18 U.S.C. §1519): Deleting content/accounts after exposure.

The conduct is continuous (spanning years) and related (all serving the fraud enterprise).

Injury to Business or Property

Potential plaintiffs include:

Consumers/Readers: Induced into subscriptions, memberships, or purchasing credibility by false credentials.

Employers/Clients: Any company retaining Johnson under false pretenses risks brand damage, regulatory exposure, and monetary loss.

Competitors: Other writers/marketers injured by false reputation-building through sockpuppet networks.

Civil RICO explicitly recognizes reputational/business harm where fraudulent enterprises distort the market (Sedima, S.P.R.L. v. Imrex Co., 473 U.S. 479 (1985)).

---

3. Remedies Available

Treble Damages: Three times actual damages proven.

Attorney’s Fees: Mandatory fee-shifting to prevailing plaintiff.

Equitable Relief: Injunctions against Joel Johnson and any sockpuppet-controlled publications or platforms.

Asset Forfeiture: Proceeds of the fraudulent enterprise can be seized.

---

4. Litigation Strategy

1. Discovery via John Doe Subpoenas:

Platforms (Medium, email hosts, ISPs) → account creation data, IP logs, payment records.

Map sockpuppet interactions to show enterprise structure.

2. Frame Sockpuppets as an Association-in-Fact Enterprise:

Show Elena, Dex, Thanion, Caleb, Deborah, etc. interacting as if independent, but controlled by Johnson.

Cite Turkette (452 U.S. 576) and Boyle (556 U.S. 938) for breadth of enterprise definition.

3. Emphasize Predicate Acts:

Each fraudulent use of credentials = wire fraud count.

Each deletion after exposure = obstruction count.

4. Argue Continuity + Relatedness:

Years-long activity.

Same fraudulent purpose: bolster reputation, discredit critics, monetize deception.

5. Plaintiffs to Target:

Any business, client, or platform that relied on his claimed credentials.

Survivors or communities misled by sockpuppet endorsements.

Consumers of his monetized publication.

---

5. Why This Case is Convincable

Enterprise exists: Sockpuppet network = association-in-fact.

Pattern exists: Multiple predicates (wire fraud, credential fraud, obstruction).

Injury exists: Consumers, companies, and communities deceived.

Causation exists: The racketeering scheme directly enabled monetization, reputation manipulation, and brand distortion.

Civil RICO is not “mafia law” alone. It is built for precisely this: fraudulent enterprises using multiple entities to deceive markets. Joel Johnson’s sockpuppet ecosystem is a textbook example.

---

📍 Bottom Line:
Joel Johnson is civilly RICO-convictable. His sockpuppet enterprise satisfies the statute’s elements, generates treble damages exposure, and creates liability not only for himself but for any company or client that benefited from his deceptive marketing methods.
