Applicable Statutes (with Rationale)

1. 18 U.S.C. §1343 — Wire Fraud

Element: Scheme to defraud, use of interstate electronic transmissions.

Fit: Johnson uses Medium, Facebook, and other platforms (interstate wires) to present fabricated credentials and plagiarized concepts as original research. This is inducement of reliance and constitutes fraud regardless of financial gain.

Case Law: United States v. Pierce, 224 F.3d 158 (2d Cir. 2000) — “scheme to defraud” includes intangible property such as reputation and intellectual credit.

2. 18 U.S.C. §1001 — False Statements

Element: Knowingly falsifying material fact within U.S. jurisdiction.

Fit: Claiming “second PhD stretch (1998–2002)” with no filed dissertation or record is a falsified material fact designed to solicit credibility in commerce and publication.

Case Law: United States v. Rodgers, 466 U.S. 475 (1984) — jurisdiction is broad where false claims influence federal or commercial processes.

3. 18 U.S.C. §1028 — Identity Fraud

Element: Knowingly using false identity or credentials to obtain value.

Fit: Adoption of academic/professional identities (“PhD,” psychologist, researcher) constitutes identity misrepresentation. Intellectual identity and academic authority fall within “means of identification” when used for advantage.

Case Law: United States v. Barrington, 648 F.3d 1178 (11th Cir. 2011) — academic fraud through false credentials upheld under §1028.

4. 18 U.S.C. §1519 — Obstruction

Element: Knowingly concealing or altering documents with intent to impede investigation.

Fit: His published “collapse” metaphor — “We do not kill what we touch, we collapse it into something measurable…alone” — demonstrates intent to reframe documentation itself as annihilation, a form of obstruction by distortion.

Case Law: Yates v. United States, 574 U.S. 528 (2015) — obstruction covers any act of concealing, altering, or falsifying information to mislead investigators.

5. 18 U.S.C. §1961 — RICO Predicate Acts

Wire fraud (§1343), false statements (§1001), identity fraud (§1028), and obstruction (§1519) are all enumerated predicates.

Continuity: Elena Byron (false psychologist), Caleb Stacey (plagiarist researcher), Thanion (fake PhD authority).
