I. The Ritual Disguised as Therapy

This is not poetry for its own sake. It is a direct parallel to a therapy room. The “bell” and “ledger” = clinical notes. The “mirror facedown” = memory hidden, truth suppressed. He signals trauma containment.

Forensic translation: He is describing a trauma session, not a ritual. The language is camouflage.

II. EMDR and the Breach of Containment

Joel explicitly references EMDR: “pulse steady, anchor named, exit mapped.”
This is a trauma protocol: the therapist anchors him before memory recall.

Forensic translation: An EMDR session triggered a sealed childhood memory. He dissociated. The trauma breached containment.

III. The Practitioner Frame

Forensic translation: He is angry at the therapist for failing to contain the memory. He reframes the clinician as incompetent, while keeping himself the one in control.
