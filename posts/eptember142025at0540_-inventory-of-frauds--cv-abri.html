🜏 INVENTORY OF FRAUDS — CV (Abridged, The Thanion Archive)

1. “Unpublished Dissertation” (Comparative Literature, 1991)

Why fraudulent: No university in the world awards a PhD without an archived dissertation. Dissertations must be deposited (ProQuest, university library, or equivalent) before degree conferral.

Self-contained collapse: The phrase “unpublished dissertation” attached to a completed PhD is a contradiction in terms. By definition, it invalidates the claim of degree.

---

2. “Logic & Quantum Information, 2002, Doctoral track” — No institution or advisors listed

Why fraudulent: Every legitimate CV lists institution, advisors, dissertation committee. Omission is not an accident — it’s concealment.

Self-contained collapse: Claiming “doctoral track” with no institution makes the credential unverifiable by design, collapsing its legitimacy.

---

3. Dual PhDs (Comparative Literature, Logic & Quantum Information)

Why fraudulent: Dual PhDs are extremely rare and require years of documentation. Presenting two doctorates without clear institutional ties is structurally impossible.

Self-contained collapse: The CV implies dual doctorate status but gives no verifiable scaffolding — no university, no dissertation, no committee, no archive.

---

4. “20+ Years in Generative AI” — claimed as early as 2005

Why fraudulent: Generative AI did not exist in usable research form until mid-2010s (GANs 2014, transformers 2017).

Self-contained collapse: Claiming professional practice in generative AI from 2005 or earlier is a chronological impossibility.

---

5. Employment history as “Thanion” pseudonym

Why fraudulent: Academic and professional CVs cannot be built under anonymous pseudonyms. Employers, publications, and universities verify identity.

Self-contained collapse: The CV presents “Thanion” as though it carries formal, verifiable employment weight. But pseudonyms cannot hold or prove credentials.

---

6. “Degree status is not relied upon as a credential”

Why fraudulent: The entire point of a CV is credential verification. Declaring that degrees are irrelevant while claiming doctorates is itself a deception.

Self-contained collapse: The statement admits the fraud: he wants authority of “doctorate” without the accountability of proof.

---

7. Chronological incoherence — 1991 Comparative Literature PhD, then 2002 Logic & Quantum track

Why fraudulent: Legitimate CVs align fields and timelines. A 1991 PhD holder would not enter an undocumented “doctoral track” a decade later with no institution listed.

Self-contained collapse: The timeline itself is incoherent and non-normative — evidence of fabrication.

---

8. Absence of institutional affiliations

Why fraudulent: Every CV lists schools, universities, labs, employers, grants. This one has none.

Self-contained collapse: The omissions are not neutral; they are necessary to prevent verification. That pattern itself is probative of fraud.

---

📍 Why this matters

Each of these is self-collapsing fraud: the CV defeats itself on its own terms. No outside investigation needed. That makes it potent evidence — easy for prosecutors, academics, and journalists to demonstrate publicly and legally.
