TL;DR

  • Problem: Nearly 90% of healthcare spending targets chronic disease, largely tied to diet and lifestyle choices.
  • Root cause: Decades of misguided guidelines prioritized grains, feared dietary fat, treated meat as optional, and focused on calories over nutrients.
  • Consequence: Missing nutrients manifest as cravings, inflammation, mood swings, and constant food novelty-seeking.
  • Solution hierarchy: Protein and fats first (repair, satiety, stability), then fruits and vegetables (micronutrients), grains last (optional fuel, not foundation).
  • Shift: Financial incentives are now aligning to drive real course-correction in nutrition science and public health.
