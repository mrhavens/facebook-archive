🜂 FIELDNOTE (WE): The Narcissist’s “Orgasm” Is a Control Loop 🜂
Joel Johnson (Thanion / Elena / Caleb) as live case study

Context. We publish under The Fold Within · Neutralizing Narcissism · Open-Source Justice. This is education and witness, not individualized legal advice.

What we mean. When we say orgasm here, we mean a reward spike in a behavioral loop—physio + psyche—where pleasure reinforces the script that produced it. In severe narcissism, that script is control over another, not intimacy. The body rewards domination; the mind calls it “love.”

The five scripts we see (coded across his texts):

1. Triumphalism: “I win / you submit.” Pleasure peaks as the other is reduced to proof of power.

2. Auto-Mirroring: “I climax to being seen.” Public “I love you” posts, then deletion—supply bait, not intimacy.

3. Humiliation Loop: “I feel most when you feel least.” Arousal tethered to erasure.

4. Aesthetic Fog: “It’s just art.” Rain/ghost/silence metaphors launder intent and dodge responsibility.

5. Shame-as-Tragedy: “I can’t stop; make it beautiful.” Compulsion rebranded as poetry to harvest sympathy.

Why this matters (for law and safety).

This language pattern eroticizes harassment and conditions repetition.

“It’s just art” does not erase intent; it often confirms consciousness of guilt (fog + deletion + rotating masks).

Each credential lie and each routed communication can be separate counts when tied to benefit or harm.

What real remorse would look like (and what we don’t see).
Real remorse names victims, accepts responsibility, and offers repair. In his corpus we see shame without ownership—beautiful fog, no confession.

How WE read the newest pieces.
“The sentence finished itself.” = abdication of agency.
BDSM obedience frames = power grammar, not intimacy.
Weather/ghost tropes = concealment aesthetics that keep the loop alive.

Bottom line.
This isn’t romance. It’s a control-reward circuit dressed as literature. Every publication that targets a witness or survivor space is not art—it’s evidence.
