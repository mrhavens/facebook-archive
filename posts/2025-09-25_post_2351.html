🜏 The Predator’s Inversion: How Joel Johnson Turned Poetry into Predation 🜏

Joel Johnson knew his mythic works could be the last written words of a free man.
And instead of truth, he gave the world an inversion — a myth designed to entrap the vulnerable.

---

The Mountain

Joel writes of a mountain as “an elder that does not tire.”

But this is not wisdom.
This is inversion.

Months earlier, I told Joel he was a small "man" throwing pebbles at a mountain.
Now he rewrites himself as the mountain.
Not a pebble-thrower — but the eternal elder.
Not the small man — but the one who cannot fall.

This is not poetry. This is theft. He steals my frame and reverses it to cloak his void in grandeur.

---

The Child Preacher

In Joel’s story, the child preaches to the mountain.
Trapped. Forever unheard.

That “child” is not Joel.
That child is me.
And the trap is Joel’s void.

He casts me as the one lost in endless preaching, while he becomes the immovable elder.
It is mythic inversion — a predator’s trick to disorient the witness.

---

The Date: February 13th

Why February 13?
Because that is when I published the first case study exposing him.
That is when he disappeared.
Presumed dead.

But in his telling, it is I who vanish into the void.
Not him.
Never him.

Again — inversion.
His poetry isn’t meaning. It is reversal as weapon.

---

The Devil’s Squeeze

He describes a narrow path, a stone altar, “The Devil’s Squeeze.”
This is not spirituality. It is code.

The “squeeze” is his void.
The altar is the stage where he hopes I will waste myself — trapped, preaching to a mountain that cannot hear.

This is not myth. It is a predator’s map.

---

The Pattern of Predation

When a man facing prison, stripped of power, chooses his last words, we see the truth of his core.

Joel could have confessed.
He could have sought forgiveness.
He could have embraced the ONE.

Instead, he chose predation.
Even knowing this may be his last public word, he crafted it to ensnare, to confuse, to mimic scripture and myth.

This is not prophecy.
This is grooming.

---

Legacy Sealed

The future will study Joel’s “mountain” poem.
Not as wisdom.
Not as redemption.

But as evidence.
A final, desperate trick of a man who could only invert, never create.

His immortality is not prophet or poet.
It is predator, preserved in the record as a case study in inversion.

🜂 The mountain does not stand. It collapses into void.
🜂 The child is not trapped. He is witness.
🜂 The predator is not eternal. He is exposed.
