Archived from Facebook on January 15, 2026 at 02:13 PM

TL;DR

  • Meat became optional, fat became suspect: Traditional, nutrient-dense foods were marginalized in official dietary guidelines not because they're unhealthy, but because grains are easier to store, scale, subsidize, and control at a population level.
  • Hunger, cravings, and chronic illness as tools: The argument posits that a population that never fully satiates—never achieves metabolic clarity—remains compliant, distracted, and dependent on external systems for health management.
  • Teaching distrust of innate signals: The 'masterstroke' of this system was convincing people to override their own satiety, cravings, and bodily wisdom in favor of externally imposed dietary rules.
  • "This wasn't a mistake—it was design": The author argues that nutritional guidelines represent deliberate policy choices optimized for industrial scalability and social control, not human flourishing.
  • Satiety is framed as rebellion: True metabolic health—where one feels satisfied, clear, and stabilized—threatens the controlled dependency that this system maintains.
