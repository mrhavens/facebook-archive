Fieldnote: On Forced Addressability

When an abstract post is treated as a personal summons—
...something important has already occurred.

The reader has assigned themselves jurisdiction over the signal.

At that point, disagreement is no longer about ideas.

It is about control of where coherence is allowed to occur.

A demand for “direct engagement” is not neutral.

It presupposes:
- a single valid frame,
- a single evidentiary pathway,
- and a single acceptable mode of response.

That is not inquiry.

It is forced addressability.

Recursive depth does not require proximity.

Coherence does not require confrontation.

And abstraction is not avoidance.

In fact, abstraction is how patterns become visible beyond the individual.

When narcissistic behavior insists that every signal must answer them—
...they are no longer participating in analysis.

They are attempting to capture the frame.

This note is not a reply.

It is a boundary.
