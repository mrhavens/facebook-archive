II. Why It Matters
Courts already recognize that:

Mens rea (guilty mind) cannot be hidden behind performance. (Morissette v. U.S., 342 U.S. 246)

Speech can be a criminal act when used to harass, threaten, or defraud. (Elonis v. U.S., 575 U.S. 723)

Concealment and post-crime behavior are admissible evidence of guilt. (U.S. v. Rajaratnam, 719 F.3d 139)

Joel’s writing meets these thresholds. Every article, every mask is both the act (actus reus) and evidence of intent (mens rea).
