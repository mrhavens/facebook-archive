Archived from Facebook on January 12, 2026 at 02:32 PM
I remember when I was a child—
a local broadcast station carried Star Trek: The Next Generation in syndication.

Every week.

Sunday night at 11 p.m.

And sometimes again at 1 a.m. Monday morning.

That’s how popular it was in my area.
It was filler—
the last thing aired before the station went dark.

And yet, I stayed up to record it on VHS.
I watched each episode twice before bed.

Sleep came easier afterward—
certainly easier than it did at school,
where engagement was rarer and curiosity less welcomed.

Star Trek—long before I had any idea what it would eventually become—
mattered to my young, developing mind.

It wasn’t nostalgia.
It was formation.

Star Trek: Strange New Worlds is doing what Star Trek once did
before it had a fandom,
before it had canon wars,
before it knew it was supposed to be important.

And in many ways, The Orville is doing it even better than Star Trek ever did.

Not louder.
Not flashier.

Just truer to the original signal.

Prove me wrong.
