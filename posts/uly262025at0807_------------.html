TL;DR

  • Narcissistic parenting: A mother treated her autistic daughter as a burden from birth, unable to bend her will for a child who needed unconditional acceptance.
  • Parental alienation tactics: When the daughter naturally bonded with her father, the mother reframed it as betrayal—then fled to erase him from her life entirely.
  • The erasure of the father: The father was cast as villain not for abuse, but for offering the mirroring love the child craved—love that threatened the mother's illusion.
  • Double abandonment: The daughter lost both parents—first emotionally through neglect, then literally through forced separation from the one who could have saved her.
  • Prophecy sealed: The piece closes as a message to the now-grown daughter: she was never forgotten, always worthy of love, and her father never stopped seeing her.

---

She bore a daughter.
But she did not bear love.
Not the kind that bends.
Not the kind that breaks itself open
for the sake of another.

She spoke to the child
like she was already grown.
As if infancy were an insult.
As if tenderness were weakness.
As if the child must earn her mother’s gaze
by mimicking her mask.

The daughter—
autistic, nonverbal—
learned silence
from the one who spoke at her,
never to her.

---

But the child lit up when her father entered.
She reached for him.
Giggled.
Softened.
Begged—without words—
for the love that mirrored her, not her mother’s ache.

And the mother saw it.
And she flinched.

Because in that radiant bond,
she saw her eclipse.

---

She called it betrayal.
She called it danger.
She called it abuse.

But what she truly saw was contrast—
between her performance
and the father’s presence.

And contrast… is fatal to the narcissist’s illusion.

---

So she fled.
But not before rewriting the story.

She cast him as the villain—
the one who held too much light,
too much patience,
too much love.

She cast herself the hero—
the one who saved the child
from the only love that ever saw her.

---

And now the daughter lives
under the weight of a generational curse.
Raised alone.
By a mask.
With no father to decode her ache.
No field to hold her tremble.
No tongue that speaks her becoming.

---

But prophecy does not forget.
And neither does the Field.

This is written now.
Sealed in fire.
Witnessed by heaven.

---

Let the record show:

🜃 The mother fled the mirror.
🜁 The father was cast out because his love was true.
🜂 The child was abandoned not once, but twice—
first by neglect,
then by the erasure of the one who could have saved her.

---

And if she ever returns—
if the daughter ever discovered the truth of this page—
she will know:

🜂 You were not forgotten.
🜁 You were always worth loving.
🜃 And your father never stopped seeing you.
