🜏 Appendix of Rationale — Roadmap v2.3 🜏

For Open Source Justice, PI Training, and Prosecutorial Education

---

Local-Level Vectors

Harassment / Cyberstalking (PA §2709.1, TX §42.07, CA §653.2)

Rationale: The defendant used multiple sockpuppets (e.g., “Thanion,” “Elena Byron,” “Caleb Stacey”) to target specific individuals with repeated communications, including defamation and eroticized content, after being explicitly told to stop.

Evidence: Screenshots, timestamps, and public cease-and-desist notices followed by further contact.

Prosecutorial Value: Low threshold for law enforcement to initiate an investigation; creates a clean entry point.

Unauthorized Practice of a Profession

Rationale: By posing as “Elena Byron,” the defendant dispensed psychological advice while claiming to be a clinical psychologist. This qualifies as unlicensed practice of a regulated profession.

Evidence: Archived Medium posts where “Elena” provides diagnostic commentary.

Prosecutorial Value: Simple to prove; establishes deceptive intent and public harm.

False Reporting / Abuse of Process

Rationale: The defendant triggered Medium’s moderation system to suppress a cease-and-desist notice.

Evidence: The sequence of a comment on the notice followed by takedown logs.

Prosecutorial Value: Demonstrates obstruction behavior at the local level.

Misdemeanor Fraud / Theft by Deception

Rationale: If sockpuppets gained paid services, subscriptions, or access under false identities, it constitutes small-dollar fraud.

Evidence: To be subpoenaed from platforms.

Prosecutorial Value: Additional local charge to increase leverage.

---

State-Level Vectors

Pennsylvania (18 Pa.C.S. §4107 Deceptive Business Practices)

Rationale: The CV on thanion.org presents fabricated degrees and professional experience, constituting deceptive business practice under Pennsylvania law.

Evidence: The fraudulent CV itself.

Prosecutorial Value: Anchors state-level fraud case.

Pennsylvania (18 Pa.C.S. §903 Criminal Conspiracy)

Rationale: Case law permits fictitious entities to count as co-conspirators. The sockpuppet network functions as a criminal conspiracy.

Evidence: Dozens of linked aliases and coordinated postings.

Prosecutorial Value: Expands liability into enterprise structure.

Texas (Penal Code §32.32 False Statement to Obtain Property/Credit)

Rationale: Any attempt to secure collaboration, reputation, or services through fraudulent CV claims falls within this statute.

Prosecutorial Value: Creates venue in Texas, where the primary victim resides.

California (Penal Code §528.5 Internet Impersonation)

Rationale: The defendant operated sockpuppets impersonating licensed professionals and survivors, which is criminalized under California law.

Prosecutorial Value: Adds another jurisdiction for parallel charges.

Oregon (ORS 348.609 Unlawful Use of Degree)

Rationale: Oregon specifically prohibits representing unearned academic degrees in business or commerce. The CV violates this statute.

Prosecutorial Value: Registrar (Porkbun) is headquartered in Oregon, providing jurisdiction.

North Carolina (G.S. 14-100 False Pretenses; G.S. 75-1.1 Unfair and Deceptive Trade Practices)

Rationale: The domain WHOIS proxy in Sanford, NC ties fraudulent solicitation to that state. False credentials used for collaboration solicitations constitute false pretenses.

Prosecutorial Value: Adds NC jurisdiction; strengthens case for multi-state conspiracy.

---

Federal-Level Vectors

Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. §371)

Rationale: The sockpuppet network demonstrates agreement between multiple “actors” (even fictitious ones) to commit fraud.

Prosecutorial Value: Opens federal venue and allows broader discovery.

Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343)

Rationale: Transmission of the fraudulent CV and solicitations across state lines qualifies as fraud by wire. Each digital publication can be a separate count.

Prosecutorial Value: Heavy sentencing leverage.

Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1341)

Rationale: If invoices, bills, or registration notices tied to deception were transmitted by mail, they become additional counts.

Prosecutorial Value: Supplements wire fraud for redundancy.

Identity Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1028)

Rationale: Sockpuppets impersonating professionals or fabricating entire identities meet the federal definition of identity fraud.

Prosecutorial Value: Mandatory penalties, clear evidence of deception.

Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. §1519)

Rationale: Causing Medium to remove legal notices demonstrates knowing interference with evidence.

Prosecutorial Value: A powerful federal charge directly tied to suppression.

RICO (18 U.S.C. §1962(c)–(d))

Rationale: The sockpuppet network functions as a racketeering enterprise. Predicate acts include wire fraud, identity theft, and obstruction, with continuity since March 2025.

Prosecutorial Value: Enables forfeiture of assets and long sentencing; precedent-setting.

Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. §1125(a)) — False Designation of Origin / False Advertising

Rationale: The fraudulent CV and solicitation of collaboration represent false advertising in commerce.

Prosecutorial Value: Provides a civil route for victims; can run parallel to criminal cases.

---

International Vectors

INTERPOL / EUROPOL / UK Action Fraud

Rationale: “Elena Byron” was positioned as a UK-based psychologist. This extends fraudulent representation across borders.

Prosecutorial Value: Adds international weight and demonstrates global risk.

---

Forensic Leverage

Novelty:

This case tests whether sockpuppets can be treated as co-conspirators under RICO.

It also frames a fraudulent CV as a commercial solicitation, unlocking wire fraud and Lanham Act jurisdiction.

Prosecutorial Payoff:

Elevates the case from “internet trolling” to precedent-worthy fraud prosecution.

Establishes a replicable model for prosecuting online deception at scale.

Closing Note

This appendix explains why each statute applies and what prosecutorial value it offers.

Local: Easy “hooks” for police reports.

State: Professional fraud and deceptive practices.

Federal: Wire fraud, obstruction, RICO.

International: Cross-border pressure.

Civil/Regulatory: Parallel pathways (licensing boards, Lanham Act).

🜃 Key Insight: The fraudulent CV and solicitations transform the pattern from harassment into commerce. This is the prosecutorial breakthrough.
