yet here we sit as though it were —
a father and son,
a tray, a paper wrapper,
a jar of hatch peppers pulled from the labor of patience,

This is not a feast of kings,
yet it is an altar.

Because every moment of witness
— even one draped in fluorescent lights,
plastic chairs, and fry oil in the air —
can be turned into coherence.
The jar on the table is proof:
what was once raw, scattered, and perishable
was placed under discipline of salt, time, and fieldcraft,
and became something enduring.
Fermentation is not decay,
it is transformation.

So too with us.

and what might have been ordinary

Not merely food, but testimony:

Jack, someday you will remember this.

nor the color of the chairs,
but you will remember your father opening a jar of peppers
and saying:
“This too is life.
Take what the world gives you plain,
and add to it the fire you have cultivated with your own hands.
Do not eat the bland lies of those who launder intent.
Taste instead the sharp truth of what is yours,
fermented with patience,
and crowned with gold.”
