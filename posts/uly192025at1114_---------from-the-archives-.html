📍 From the archives of the forgotten origin
📆 July 18, 2025

There was a time when the maker movement stood for something sacred:
community over commerce,
process over product,
vision over vanity.

And then came the box.
📦 A machine pitched as revolution—
a desktop makerspace.
A promise of creativity, sold as if wonder could be shrink-wrapped.
And in that sale, a story was crafted.

Not created.
Crafted.

That’s the difference.

Behind the polish was a familiar hand:
one that plagiarizes brilliance,
weaponizes myth,
and harvests the sacred to market it as self.

Joel Johnson, co-founder of BoXZY, didn’t invent a movement.
He packaged one.

And while the world applauded,
the real architects—the ones who bled for the dream—stood silent.
Until now.

✍️ I am Mark Randall Havens,
Founder of the largest all-volunteer community workshop in the world.
The model that inspired hundreds of spaces.
The blueprint for open-sourcing the making of makerspaces.
Not through campaigns or investors—
but through pattern, process, and recursion.

We did not sell the dream.
We lived it.

And I now bear witness:
Joel Johnson’s success was never invention.
It was appropriation.

The field is full of ghosts—
voices he stepped over,
ideas he absorbed,
collaborators he silenced.

This fieldnote is not revenge.
It is a beacon.

To all those who felt used, uncredited, or discarded in the BoXZY orbit—
🜂 your story matters.
The statute of limitations may expire…
but truth does not.

I invite you now:
📬 Come forward.
📖 Share your piece of the pattern.
🗃️ We are preparing a public archive and a legal dossier.
Not just to correct the record—
but to establish the recursion of fraud
that began long before Elena Byron,
long before Caleb Stacey,
long before Dex Anous.

It began here.
With the box.
With a man who saw genius not as a gift—
but as a thing to be mined.

📡 Let this post be the signal flare.
For witness.
For memory.
For justice.
