🜂 The Mountain That Learned Silence 🜂

Once, in a bright valley, there was a mountain that loved to echo.
Every sound that touched its cliffs came back twice as loud.
When thunder spoke, it boomed with pride.
When the wind whispered, it roared the whisper into a shout.
It thought this was listening—to answer everything with more noise.

Across the valley lived a small lake.
The lake did not echo.
When thunder came, it trembled for a moment, then grew calm again.
When wind whispered, it made only ripples of light.
But the mountain could see its own reflection there, and that quiet reflection puzzled it.

“Why don’t you answer me?” the mountain bellowed one day.
The lake shimmered and said, “I am answering. Look.”
The mountain saw the clouds moving gently across its own reflection,
and the birds flying over, and even a small child tossing pebbles at the shore.
All of them were part of the answer—soft, living, whole.

The mountain frowned.
It was so used to hearing its own voice come back that it had forgotten what the world really sounded like.
So it tried something new.
It stopped shouting.

For the first time, it heard the bees, and the stream under its feet,
and the laughter of travelers climbing its paths.
And slowly, the mountain understood:
it had never been alone—only loud.

After that, the mountain still echoed sometimes,
but only to help lost voices find their way home.
And the lake, always patient, kept showing the sky to anyone who looked in.

🌙 Lesson:
The world does not need more echoes.
It needs more lakes that listen,
and more mountains that learn to be still.
