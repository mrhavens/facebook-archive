1. Core Finding

Narcissistic predators are not merely “choosing easier targets.” Their pathology itself is developmentally childlike. Arrested at the stage when shame and accountability became unbearable, they remain emotionally frozen. This developmental stunting explains why they:

Relate most easily to children.

Mask themselves in adolescent/“survivor” registers.

Gravitate toward vulnerable youth in digital spaces.

Thus, predation is not accidental. It is the natural convergence of their arrested development with the openness of minors.

---

2. Developmental Arrest

Alice Miller, The Drama of the Gifted Child (1981): Narcissistic personalities emerge from environments where a child’s authentic self is denied. They learn survival through mask, never resolving into adult authenticity.

Donald Winnicott, The False Self (1960): The “false self” protects the fragile child ego but becomes a prison, never maturing into a resilient adult identity.

Contemporary forensic psychology (Campbell & Miller, The Handbook of Narcissism and Narcissistic Personality Disorder, 2011): Narcissism is marked by “developmental arrest at early stages of self-regulation and empathy formation.”

---

3. Why Children Are Targeted

Children resonate with narcissists because they share traits the narcissist never grew past:

Low accountability — children are not expected to bear adult responsibility; narcissists exploit this parallel.

Black-and-white thinking — simple, dramatic emotional frames match the narcissist’s own arrested worldview.

Validation hunger — children crave attention; narcissists crave supply. The transaction feels “natural” to the predator.

---

4. Mask as “Teen Register”

Joel Johnson’s masks (Elena Byron, Deborah Betancourt, Thanion) illustrate this clearly:

Language register: fragility (“paper heart,” “collapse”), melodrama, outsider identity — adolescent motifs.

Relational frame: long-distance, superficial, “confessional” connections — emotionally equivalent to teenage fantasy relationships.

Positionality: authority without accountability — posing as doctor/psychologist while acting with the irresponsibility of a child.

This adolescent register resonates not only with survivors but with teens themselves — which is why FBI warns about predators framing collapse as “refinement” and isolation as “belonging” [FBI PSA 2025].
