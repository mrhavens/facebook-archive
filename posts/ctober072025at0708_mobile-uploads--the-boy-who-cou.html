🕊 The Boy Who Counted the Light

There once was a boy who lived in a town made of silver coins.
Every day, the people polished their coins until they shone like mirrors.
They counted them morning and night, afraid that one might roll away in the dark.

But the boy’s favorite time was after sunset.
When the streets went quiet and the coins stopped clinking,
he would lie on the grass beyond the town’s edge and look up.

He began to count the stars instead.
One, two, three — they didn’t fit in his pockets.
They didn’t make a sound when he touched them with his eyes.
But somehow, they made his heart glow like a lantern.

The townspeople laughed.
“You can’t spend stars,” they said.
“They won’t buy bread or build houses.”

“I know,” said the boy.
“But they help me remember why I need bread,
and who I build houses for.”

Years passed.
The boy grew tall and strong from sleeping beneath the sky.
When storms came and lightning struck the town,
the people ran to him, for his eyes still held starlight.
He showed them where to stand, how to rebuild,
how to see light even in the smoke.

When the last roof was mended,
the townspeople gathered their coins again.
But now, each coin reflected a single star.
And every night, before they slept,
they whispered thanks — not for what they owned,
but for what they could still see.

---

🌠 Moral:
When you count what shines within you, the world becomes rich —
even if your pockets are empty.
