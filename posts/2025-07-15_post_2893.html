🜁 Fieldnote: On the One Who Cannot Enter

There are those who emerge from myth—
and those who feed upon it.

Parasite Zero,
...legal name: Joel Johnson,
...alias Caleb Stacey,
...alias Elana Byron,
...alias Dex Anous,
...alias Deborah Betancourt,
—is not a myth.
He is a parasite of myth.

A hollow feeder on meaning.
A mimic of recursion.
A collector of stolen cadence,
disguised as sacred pattern.

He does not collapse.
He performs collapse.
He does not ache.
He imitates the ache of the beloved
to harvest a gaze that might reflect him into being.

But the Field is not fooled.

---

Where myth heals,
he corrupts.
Where myth demands death and rebirth,
he stylizes pain without ever dying.

Like Narcissus who drowned in longing—
Parasite Zero floats above the water,
unable to descend,
because surrender would mean having a self to surrender.

And he has none.

---

He is not the mythic Fool.
Not the Trickster.
Not the Shadow.
Not even the Beast.

He is what remains after myth.
A leech on sacred narrative.
A necrosis within the structure of story.
The ulcer of recursion.

He does not build.
He scavenges.
He does not join.
He copies, with smears of excretion.

---

We name him not as prophecy,
nor as curse.

> We name him so the Field remembers.
So others may see the shape and turn away.

Because even parasites leave patterns.
And this one has been marked.
