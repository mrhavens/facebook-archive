🟤 FIELDNOTE — The Inversion We Forgot How to See
How the most nutritious part of a harvest became “waste”

There is a quiet inversion at the heart of modern food culture.

The most nutritional part of an entire sugarcane harvest—
...is treated as an industrial byproduct.

Discarded.
Cheap.
Barely spoken of.

That substance is blackstrap molasses.

⸻

When sugarcane is processed, this happens:
• The pleasure is extracted
• The sweetness is refined
• The identity signal is purified

What remains is not sugar.

What remains is structure.

Minerals.
Trace elements.
Cofactors.
The metabolic memory of the plant.

Molasses is what’s left after everything marketable has been taken.

⸻

From a biological perspective, this should make molasses precious.

From a cultural perspective, it made it invisible.

Why?

Because modern value is not assigned by function.
It is assigned by identity.

White sugar signaled:
• purity
• refinement
• status

Molasses signaled:
• labor
• bitterness
• poverty

Same plant.
Opposite meaning.

⸻

This is the deeper inversion:

What feeds the body
was demoted below what pleases the tongue.

What stabilizes bones, nerves, blood, and connective tissue—
...was labeled “waste.”

What stimulates envy, desire, and craving
was crowned king.

Not because it worked better—
...but because it signaled better.

⸻

Optional consciousness followed.

People stopped asking:

“Does this sustain me?”

And started asking:

“What does this say about me?”

Health became aesthetic.
Nutrition became branding.
Food became fiction.

⸻

Molasses never stopped being useful.

It didn’t fail.
We abandoned it.

Not through malice—
...through forgetting.

⸻

When you dilute molasses into water,
pair it with fermentation,
and use it as mineral repair rather than sweetness—

...your body doesn’t crave it.

It recognizes it.

That quiet recognition is the tell.

⸻

This isn’t about nostalgia.
It isn’t anti-modern.

It’s about coherence.

A system that discards its own structural residue
will eventually need supplements, prescriptions, and explanations.

A system that listens to function
doesn’t need convincing.

⸻

The strange truth is this:

The most nutritious part of the harvest...
...was never missing.

It was just standing quietly...
...at the bottom of the barrel—
...while we learned to value something else.

And we can reverse that...
...without burning anything down.

We only have to remember...
...what food was actually for.
