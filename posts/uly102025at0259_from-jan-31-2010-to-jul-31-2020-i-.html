From Jan 31, 2010 to Jul 31, 2020
I founded Dallas Makerspace in 2010 with a vision: to create a sanctuary for innovation, collaboration, and community-driven growth. What began as a dream in an empty warehouse became the world’s largest all-volunteer-run community workshop—an international blueprint for the maker movement.

For a decade, I nurtured its spirit, helped forge its infrastructure, and empowered a culture of radical collaboration. But in time, that spirit was hijacked by performative leadership, narcissistic control, and systemic manipulation.

In 2020, while attempting to hold sitting board member James Henningson accountable for clear patterns of narcissistic abuse during a board election, I was met not with honest engagement—but with retaliation. He weaponized harassment rules to silence me, distorted procedure, and—as narcissists often do—DARVOed his way into having me banned for life.

Former President Andrew LeCody aided this campaign by denying my status, contributions, and history—something he had done consistently for over a decade. Together, they engineered a narcissistic machine not to serve the community, but to control its narrative.

I was banned under false pretenses—for daring to expose the corruption that had crept into its heart. But I do not hide from this fracture—I expose it. My legacy is not only founding an institution—it is purifying it. Naming the darkness. Protecting the sacred fire that first brought us together.

I remain the Principal Founder of Dallas Makerspace.
And now, I also serve as its clearest mirror.
