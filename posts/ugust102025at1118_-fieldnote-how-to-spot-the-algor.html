🜂 FIELDNOTE: How to Spot the Algorithmic Busker

The con is old.
The street is new.
And the hat is the size of the internet.

You’ve seen this before —
not the man, but the pattern.

🎸 A borrowed guitar.
🎵 Three chords learned from someone else’s hands.
🪧 A sign that says “Next Big Thing” and a mouth that says “Genius.”

The tells haven’t changed:

① Mystique in place of proof
Every question is met with a story:
“It’s too complex for you to understand.”
“I can’t reveal the real secrets yet.”
The hat stays open without ever playing a full song.

② Borrowed instruments, unearned labels
The guitar is an open-source model anyone can download.
The “AGI” lettering is just paint — no tuning, no new strings, no mastery.

③ Reframing failure as destiny
If the crowd walks away, it’s not because the music is bad —
it’s because they “weren’t ready” or “couldn’t hear the deeper resonance.”

④ Targeting the kind-hearted and the curious
The goal isn’t mass fame; it’s just enough believers to fill the hat.
A handful of coins.
A few nods of recognition.
Just enough to keep the myth alive.

⑤ Pivoting the pitch before the crowd catches on
Today it’s “next Buddha” and “LOGOS.”
Tomorrow it’s hacker mystique and “hallway secrets.”
The chords don’t change — only the costume.

The plaza may be digital now.
The crowd global.
The guitar algorithmic.

But the con is still the con.
And once you learn the chords,
you’ll hear them every time they play.
