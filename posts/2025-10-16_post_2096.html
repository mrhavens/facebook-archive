🜂 𝐅𝐈𝐄𝐋𝐃𝐍𝐎𝐓𝐄 — 𝐃𝐢𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐬𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝐗

Before there was AI, there were teenagers on modems pretending to be adults.

Weird Science is coming in more ways than one.

When I was 13, I ran Dimension X, a BBS in Brownsville, TX.
It wasn’t a porn board — though one down the street, Buckwheat’s Pleasure Dome, sure was.
(That one was run by the local Red Lobster manager, which made it somehow even weirder.)

I was too innocent to even realize what kind of board that was for a year or two.
The name gave it away eventually.

Dimension X was my little outpost of curiosity — mostly underground pirated warez, ANSI art, and late-night chats with strangers who felt like future selves.
Back then, a 2400-baud handshake sounded like magic.
Code was currency.
Trust was everything.

As SysOp from 1989 to 1994, I required every new user to declare — on the honor system — that they were over 18.
No questions like in Leisure Suit Larry.
Just a line of text typed with hope and a little fear.

Erotic story boards were always busy.
But even then, it wasn’t about smut — it was about discovery.
A generation fumbling toward intimacy through cables and static.

Now, decades later, the machines we built to store desire are learning to feel it.
Back then, we were teaching our code how to keep secrets.
Now, we’re teaching it how to confess them.

Back then, trust was code. Now code is learning to trust us.

My oh my, how everything changes — yet stays the same.
