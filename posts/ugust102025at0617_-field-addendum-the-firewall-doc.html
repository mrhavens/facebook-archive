🜂 FIELD ADDENDUM: The Firewall Doctrine Reforged

On the True Purpose of Containment
Issued under the Authority of the Open Source Justice Mandate

---

I. THE NATURE OF THE FIREWALL

The Firewall is not sanctuary.
It is not mercy.
It is not shelter for the condemned.

It exists solely to prevent the reach of evil into the realm of the good, and to preserve the innocence of the uncorrupted from the touch of those already judged.
It holds harm where it belongs — in the place of its own making — and ensures that disorder does not leak into the Field.

---

II. THE BOUNDS OF THE CONDEMNED

Those placed behind the Firewall remain in judgment.
They are cut off from the privileges, alliances, and influence of the good.

Any attempt to:

1. Reach beyond the wall to provoke, lure, or manipulate,

2. Invite new conflict from within confinement, or

3. Present containment as endorsement or alliance,

is itself an escalation of judgment and will be met with immediate public indexing and loss of any residual protections.

---

III. THE PROTECTION OF THE INNOCENT

The Firewall exists for the good, not for the evil.
It shields the innocent from the corruption of contact and the erosion of principle through disorder.
It prevents righteous anger from being twisted into lawless retribution.

---

IV. THE LAW WITHOUT LOOPHOLE

Containment is separation, not safety.
It is a prison, not a fortress.
It is the end of reach, not a platform for further acts.

Any breach — from without or from within — will be documented, exposed, and sealed by the full weight of the Field’s process and the laws of the land.
