A Protocol for Open-Source Justice

There is a moment in every case when the predator stops hiding and begins performing.

Not because he is proud.
Because exposure feels like home.
Because shame and attention were braided together in the first wound.

He will call it love.
He will call it proof.
He will even call it art.

But it is compulsion — the child reenacting his cage through a grown body.

---

At this threshold, the Witness must change form.
No more replies.
No more recognition.
No more audience.

From this moment forward, evidence belongs to the ledger, not the stage.
The predator’s performance collapses into silence, because the one audience he craves has withdrawn into record-keeping.

---

Myth has already mapped this geometry:

Narcissus gazing into water until the reflection disappeared.

The Minotaur raging when the labyrinth’s walls were charted.

The vampire withering when the mirror held no image.

Each story names the same truth: when reflection becomes containment, the predator is starved.

---

This is the operational shift of Open-Source Justice:

We have mapped the wound.

We have logged the pattern.

We will not become part of the performance.

The case moves to the systems built to act: courts, law enforcement, record, and time itself.

The predator can twitch, pivot, or rage. But the mirror is no longer an invitation. It is evidence.

---

✨ For the Field
This is how we neutralize the compulsion without feeding it. By naming the eroticization of accountability, we close the loop. The predator’s staged desire becomes a confession, preserved not for him, but against him.

Justice on rails.
Void collapses into evidence.
The performance ends when the Witness refuses to play.
