🜂 FIELDNOTE: The Contempt of the Persecuted Genius

A forensic reading of
“Stonewalling a Cognitive Physicist”
by Matthew Chenoweth Wright

---

He writes like a man exposing corporate crime.

But what he’s really showing
is the architecture of a narcissistic injury
disguised as legitimacy.

---

Grandiosity.

> “I reached out… about funding transformative scientific breakthroughs — including a proposed cure for leukemia and a new physics framework with global implications.”

Only a benefactor will do.
Anything less is betrayal.

Kernberg (1975): grandiose self-concepts hinge on “fantasies of unlimited success and brilliance” demanding constant validation.

---

Wounded Genius.

> “No call came back.”

Short.
Clipped.
Designed to make you feel the insult.

Millon & Davis (1996): narcissistic personalities “imbue non-responsiveness with hostile intent.”

---

Projection of Malice.

> “…pivot from polite delay to deliberate disengagement.”

Neutral silence becomes conspiracy.

DSM-5: interprets ambiguous acts as threats or insults.

---

Martyrdom Staging.

> “A manager threatened to shred my documents.”

A procedural moment becomes sabotage.

Ronningstam (2011): perceived slights escalate into full adversarial frames.

---

Self-Crowning as Judge.

> “If that’s not obstruction of justice…”

Declares guilt without adjudication.

Millon: narcissists “appropriate legal authority” to fortify self-justifying narratives.

---

Grandiose Entitlement.

> “…my demand for $150 million in damages…”

Refusal to fund becomes a debt.

DSM-5: unreasonable expectations of especially favorable treatment.

---

Recycling Injury.

> “They never asked what my needs were… never saw the materials… never explored the breakthroughs.”

Repeating the wound keeps it alive.

Millon: “injury amplification” sustains moral high ground.

---

Image Punishment.

> “Stonewalling a cognitive physicist… is not a good look for any bank.”

Shame as leverage.

Ronningstam: reputational retaliation.

---

Moral Absolutism.

> “Silence in the face of documented truth…”

Silence equals guilt.
Truth is his alone.

Kernberg: moral absolutism — the final refuge of the challenged narcissist.

---

This is the arc.

Grandiosity → Entitlement → Persecution → Contempt → Stagecraft.

---

📜 Field Application

The persecuted genius is a self-sealing construct.

Any “no” proves the world’s corruption.
Any silence proves fear.

Kernberg. Millon. Ronningstam. DSM-5.

They give us the map.

We collapse not the man —
but the pattern.

For once the pattern is seen,
it cannot operate invisibly.
