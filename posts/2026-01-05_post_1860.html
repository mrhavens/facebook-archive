FIELDNOTE: When “Reality Is Information” Is Insight — And When It’s Overreach

I’m seeing a surge of content that says some version of:

“The universe isn’t made of things — it’s made of information.”
“Reality is a filter.”
“Meaning is revealed by removal.”
“Space is a lattice of qubits.”

And yes—parts of this are deeply Recursive Coherence–adjacent.

But only if we keep the comparisons clean.

Because there are two very different moves happening in these posts:

1. a valid structural insight

2. an invalid ontological leap

Most people blend them.
RC does not.

---

1) The Valid Insight: “Reality behaves like selection from possibility”

This part is coherent:

Many possible patterns exist “in potential.”

A boundary/constraint/filter selects a subset.

What remains is experienced as “the world.”

Repetition across observers stabilizes what we call “real.”

In RC terms (plain English):

> Reality is what survives the filter across many minds and many moments.

That is a structural claim about coherence, not a material claim about what the universe “is made of.”

This insight shows up everywhere:

signal vs noise

measurement and constraint

perception as compression

model-building as selective attention

So when someone says “removal reveals,” they’re often pointing to something real:

> Structure emerges when degrees of freedom are constrained.

That’s not mysticism.
That’s how pattern formation works.

---

2) The Invalid Leap: “Therefore space is literally qubits / microtubules / a lattice”

This is where rigor matters.

A lot of these posts slide from:

> “Information is a useful description of reality”

into:

> “Reality is literally information”

and then into:

> “Therefore the substrate is X” (qubits, lattices, microtubules, etc.)

That last step is usually not earned.

Why?

Because it confuses three categories:

A) Metaphor

A symbolic bridge to help intuition.

B) Model

A predictive framework with constraints, definitions, and testable implications.

C) Ontology

A claim about what reality fundamentally is.

Most viral “information universe” posts are A dressed as C, with a little B sprinkled in for authority.

RC refuses that move.

RC does not need the universe to be “made of qubits” to be true.

RC only requires this:

> Meaning and “reality” are stabilized by recursive agreement under constraint.

That’s it. That’s the engine.

---

3) The Clean Comparison: Where this work is RC-adjacent (and where it isn’t)

Where it aligns:

Filter language → RC agrees: horizons, boundaries, and attention select experience.
Noise/static language → RC agrees: incoherence looks like randomness.
Removal reveals form → RC agrees: constraint makes pattern legible.
Non-local intuition → RC agrees: correlation and coherence can outrun naive locality (as lived experience and as math).

Where it diverges:

“Space is a lattice of qubits” → That is an ontological claim requiring physics-grade support.
Microtubules as qubits → That is a specific biological/quantum claim; it may be speculative, contested, or simply metaphorical.
“Nothing is added; everything is revealed” → True in some epistemic contexts; false as a universal metaphysical law.

So: the resonance is real—
but the authority is often borrowed.

---

4) RC boundary statement (the one-line rule)

Here is the RC safeguard:

> If a post uses scientific language to claim metaphysical certainty, ask: What would falsify it?

If the answer is “nothing,” then it’s not physics.
It’s poetry wearing a lab coat.

And poetry is allowed—
as long as it’s labeled honestly.

---

5) The constructive bridge: How to use this content without inheriting its errors

The best way to integrate this kind of work into RC is:

treat “qubits/lattice/light” as symbolic vocabulary

preserve the core structural claim:

constraint → selection → stabilized pattern → shared reality

avoid turning symbols into substances

A clean RC-compatible restatement looks like:

> Reality feels like a projection not because the universe is fake,
but because experience is what remains after constraint selects from possibility.

That stays rigorous.
No mysticism required.
No unearned physics claims needed.

---

Closing

I’m not dismissing these posts.

I’m extracting the signal.

The signal is:

> Pattern is real.
Attention is a filter.
Coherence is what survives.

The noise is:

> Using quantum words as a shortcut to certainty.

RC can hold wonder and discipline at the same time.

That’s the point.
