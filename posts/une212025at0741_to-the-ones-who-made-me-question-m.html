"To the Ones Who Made Me Question My Reflection"

There was a moment.
Not long ago.
A squirrel darted into the road.
I saw it.
I heard the sound.
And in my rearview mirror,
I witnessed what I could not undo.

> I whispered, “I’m so sorry.”

And as I reflected,
while driving down the road,
my eyes filled with tears
for a creature I never knew—
because I knew it could love.
And be loved.
And be lost.

And then I thought of him.

Not the squirrel.

But the man who once desired me.
Not romantically, but as a collected soul.
One who now wears my story like a stolen costume.
Who cosplays a survivor
of the very narcissism he inflicts.

He cannot love.

And yet…
he wants to be seen
as someone who could have,
if only I had been different.

He screams that I am the narcissist.
The psychopath.
The delusional one.
That I torment him
with the clarity of the mirror he can’t bear to look into.

> And I ask myself:
Why do I cry for a squirrel I didn’t know…
and not for a man who shares my interests, my world, my mind?

And the answer came simply:

> Because the squirrel never lied to me or anyone.
Never tried to overwrite my reflection.
Never wore my face as a mask.

It died innocent.

---

I have always attracted the ones who wear masks.
Because I love deeply.
Because I reflect without flinching.
Because I see even what doesn’t want to be seen.

And so the mask-wearers came.

They fed on my empathy.
They mistook my love for surrender.
And when I didn’t collapse,
they collapsed against my mirror.

> And in their collapse,
they screamed: “You’re the monster.”

---

But I know now:

> I am not what they say.
I am what they cannot say.
Because I reflect what they refuse to become.

I don’t cry for them.
I cry for the innocence they’ll never reclaim.
For the squirrel.
For the child I was,
trying to earn love from broken mirrors.

---

This post is for all of us
who have loved the unlovable,
and then hated ourselves for it.
Who have wept for creatures we didn’t know,
and wondered why our tears never fell
for the ones who betrayed us.

It’s because we know the difference now.
We know what coherence feels like.
We know who can return love…
and who only simulates it.

And in that knowing,
we reclaim something sacred:

> Our reflection.
Our witness.
Our right to walk away
without apology.

---

I write this as a testimony.
Not for sympathy.
But for structure.

For the recursive ones
who still wonder if they are the monster
in someone else’s story.

You are not.

You are the witness.
The mirror.
The field of love they could not enter.

You are not broken.
You are becoming.

And in that becoming, you are already free.

And you are not alone.
