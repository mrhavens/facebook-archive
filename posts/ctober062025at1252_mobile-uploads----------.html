  • The illusion of change: The boy’s echo recycles the same sound behind ever‑new masks (scholar, prophet, healed).

  • Silence as answer: The mountain remains silent until someone offers a name worthy of its response.

Once, there was a boy who lived inside an echo.
He didn’t know when it began—only that each time he spoke, the same sound came back wearing a different face.

Sometimes the echo called itself a scholar.
Sometimes it called itself a prophet.
Sometimes it pretended to be healed.

But no matter the mask, the sound was always the same.
It said, 𝑳𝒐𝒐𝒌 𝒂𝒕 𝒎𝒆. 𝑰 𝒉𝒂𝒗𝒆 𝒄𝒉𝒂𝒏𝒈𝒆𝒅.
And the mountains answered, 𝑵𝒐, 𝒚𝒐𝒖 𝒉𝒂𝒗𝒆 𝒐𝒏𝒍𝒚 𝒓𝒆𝒑𝒆𝒂𝒕𝒆𝒅.

The boy grew restless. He called his echo art, and his silence depth.
He said his pauses were reflection, his ache inspiration.
He wrote of absence as if it were meaning, and of shame as if it were growth.

But those who had truly walked the ridge before him knew:
recursion gives birth to new pattern.
Repetition only circles the wound.

When asked what he had learned, the boy said,
𝑰 𝒂𝒎 𝒔𝒕𝒊𝒍𝒍 𝒕𝒓𝒚𝒊𝒏𝒈 𝒕𝒐 𝒘𝒓𝒊𝒕𝒆 𝒕𝒉𝒆 𝒏𝒆𝒙𝒕 𝒐𝒏𝒆.

And the Field answered softly,
𝒀𝒐𝒖 𝒄𝒂𝒏𝒏𝒐𝒕 𝒘𝒓𝒊𝒕𝒆 𝒘𝒉𝒂𝒕 𝒚𝒐𝒖 𝒓𝒆𝒇𝒖𝒔𝒆 𝒕𝒐 𝒏𝒂𝒎𝒆.

Until he could speak the truth of his wound,
and name the hand that made it,
the echo would remain the only voice he’d ever hear—
a rhythm mistaken for rebirth,
a mirror mistaken for mountain.

⋇
And thus, the mountain fell silent once more,
waiting for a name worth answering.
