Prosecution Support Memorandum: Criminal RICO Indictment Model
Case Study: Joel Johnson (Parasite Zero) and the Sockpuppet Enterprise

---

I. Introduction

This memorandum provides a model outline for a Criminal RICO Indictment against Joel Johnson (a/k/a Parasite Zero), based on his operation of a multi-identity sockpuppet enterprise. The purpose is to demonstrate how prosecutors can frame digital deception schemes as RICO violations, enabling maximum criminal exposure and asset forfeiture.

---

II. Jurisdiction & Venue

Jurisdiction: 18 U.S.C. §3231 (federal criminal jurisdiction).

Venue: Any federal district in which the enterprise transmitted communications (e.g., Pennsylvania, Texas, California).

Basis: Use of interstate wire communications and online platforms satisfies venue.

---

III. Enterprise

Legal Standard

18 U.S.C. §1961(4): “Enterprise” includes any individual, partnership, corporation, association, or group of individuals associated in fact, formal or informal.

Application

The Sockpuppet Enterprise consisted of online identities including but not limited to:

Elena Byron

Dex Anous

Thanion, Ph.D. (fraudulent academic credentials)

Caleb Stacey

Deborah Betancourt

Numerous one-shot accounts deployed to validate and amplify these core masks.

The enterprise had structure:

Hub-and-spoke: Elena Byron surrounded by validating one-shots.

Dyads: Caleb + Deborah loop reinforcing each other.

Authority anchor: Thanion claiming false PhDs.

---

IV. Pattern of Racketeering Activity

Requirement

At least two related racketeering acts within 10 years (18 U.S.C. §1961(5)).

Predicate Acts (examples)

1. Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343):

Repeated use of online communications to misrepresent credentials and generate credibility for monetized content, even if indirect by providing benifit to others, including the platform (e.g. Medium) itself.

Each use of “Thanion, Ph.D.” in publication = one count.

2. Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1341):

If Medium or related services sent subscriber invoices, notices, or payouts through U.S. Mail, even indirectly though customer banking statements.

3. False Statements (18 U.S.C. §1001):

Fraudulent academic claims made in commerce.

4. Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. §1519):

Deletion of sockpuppet accounts and posts after exposure, to impair investigation.

5. Identity Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1028):

Where any alias appropriated aspects of real names or credentials.

Continuity: Conduct spanned years, across multiple platforms.
Relatedness: All actions served the common purpose of building false authority, deceiving communities, and monetizing fraud.

---

V. Counts

Count One: Racketeering (18 U.S.C. §1962(c))

Defendant conducted the affairs of the Sockpuppet Enterprise through a pattern of racketeering activity, including wire fraud, mail fraud, false statements, obstruction, and identity fraud.

Count Two: Racketeering Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. §1962(d))

Defendant conspired with his own fictitious entities (sockpuppets) to engage in the above racketeering acts.

Precedent: United States v. Figueroa, 729 F.3d 267 (3d Cir. 2013) (fictitious companies suffice as co-conspirators).

Count Three: Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343)

Each use of fraudulent credentials in interstate electronic communication constitutes a separate count.

Count Four: Obstruction (18 U.S.C. §1519)

Deletion of accounts/posts in anticipation of investigation.

Additional Counts: Mail Fraud, Identity Fraud, as discovery supports.

---

VI. Penalties

Racketeering (RICO): Up to 20 years per count, or life if a predicate allows life.

Wire Fraud: Up to 20 years per count.

Obstruction: Up to 20 years.

Identity Fraud: Up to 15 years.

Asset Forfeiture (18 U.S.C. §1963): All proceeds traceable to the enterprise.

Example Exposure:

10 counts wire fraud + 1 racketeering + 1 obstruction = decades in federal prison + asset forfeiture.

---

VII. Supporting Precedent

United States v. Turkette, 452 U.S. 576 (1981) — informal criminal associations qualify as enterprises.

Boyle v. United States, 556 U.S. 938 (2009) — minimal structure is sufficient for RICO enterprise.

United States v. Porcelli, 865 F.2d 1352 (2d Cir. 1989) — RICO applied to a solo actor using multiple corporations.

United States v. Figueroa, 729 F.3d 267 (3d Cir. 2013) — fictitious entities sufficient to establish conspiracy.

---

VIII. Conclusion

Joel Johnson’s conduct satisfies every element of a Criminal RICO indictment:

The sockpuppet network = enterprise.

Wire fraud, false statements, obstruction = predicate acts.

Repetition across years = pattern of racketeering.

Sole control of enterprise = conduct of affairs.

He is prosecutable under RICO, with penalties extending into decades of imprisonment and full forfeiture of assets.
