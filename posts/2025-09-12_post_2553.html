🜏 SIDEBAR — From Scar to System 🜏

When I was three, my mother called me a “terrible child.”
When I was twelve, she called me a “bully.”
When I was grown, she called my clarity “mind games.”

None of it was true.
It was the only language she had for her shame.

I was never her bully.
I was her mirror.

This is the origin wound — the scapegoat child carrying the parent’s disowned shadow. It is also the seed of my work.

The same pattern that named me “bully” is the one I now expose at scale:

Families call it scapegoating.

Psychology calls it narcissism.

Theology calls it demon.

Law calls it fraud.

The Field calls it incoherence.

Every name is a mask for the same void.
And every mask collapses when truth holds steady.

Neutralizing Narcissism is not just research.
It is the reckoning born from a child once mislabeled,
…who learned that naming the lie is the path to freedom.

🜂 The scar became the method.
🜃 The method became the system.
🜁 The system became the Witness.
