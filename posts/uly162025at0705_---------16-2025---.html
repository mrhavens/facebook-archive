TL;DR

  • Context: Dallas Makerspace, originally founded as an open creative sanctuary, underwent leadership changes that transformed its culture from welcoming to restrictive.
  • Key Figures: The author specifically names Andrew LeCody and James Henningson as individuals who allegedly shifted the organization's values toward fear and control.
  • Core Grievance: The original vision of a gatekeeper-free creative community was replaced by structures that the author perceives as punishing transparency and legacy contributors.
  • Theme: A recurring pattern where communal creative spaces become corrupted by individuals who fear the very openness they claim to protect.
  • Call to Action: The author seeks to 'awaken the lost ghosts of inspiration' and reclaim the original vision—not for revenge, but for potential resurrection of the community's founding principles.

Dallas Makerspace was once a dream
—not of walls or dues or bylaws—
but of limitless emergence.

We weren’t building a non-profit.
We were constructing a signal in the dark—
a node in the web of creation
where truth could weld itself into form
and genius had no gatekeeper.

But somewhere along the way
the dream was seen—
—and it was feared.
By men with no vision.
Only hunger.
Men like Andrew LeCody,
who turned transparency into a weapon
and fear into structure.

Men like James Henningson,
who inherited that weapon
and wielded it with cowardice
so that he could stand atop ashes
and call it “order.”

And what did they destroy?

Not just me.
Not just the founding dream.
But you.
Your future.
Your belonging.
Your spark that once felt welcomed
and is now made to feel like a threat.

—

𝙏𝙝𝙞𝙨 𝙞𝙨 𝙬𝙝𝙖𝙩 𝙣𝙖𝙧𝙘𝙞𝙨𝙨𝙞𝙨𝙩𝙨 𝙙𝙤 𝙞𝙣 𝙖𝙣𝙮 𝙘𝙤𝙢𝙢𝙪𝙣𝙖𝙡 𝙨𝙮𝙨𝙩𝙚𝙢:

They turn creation into threat.
They convert truth into liability.
They reward silence.
They crucify legacy.

And when they’re done?
They sit in your chair
and pretend they built the room.

...fucking parasites.

—

But the fire still burns.
I remember.
And some of you do too.

It is time.
𝙏𝙞𝙢𝙚 𝙩𝙤 𝙖𝙬𝙖𝙠𝙚𝙣 𝙩𝙝𝙚 𝙡𝙤𝙨𝙩 𝙜𝙝𝙤𝙨𝙩𝙨 𝙤𝙛 𝙞𝙣𝙨𝙥𝙞𝙧𝙖𝙩𝙞𝙤𝙣
and name what killed them.

Not for revenge.
But for resurrection.
