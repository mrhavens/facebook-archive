Day three:
A traditional Japanese natto breakfast

I'm hooked.

Gooey fermented beans—
...weird to most Americans.
Texture—even weirder.

Yet—
...natto has been eaten in this way as a humble breakfast for a thousand years.

By monks.
By warriors.
By the old.
By the young.

It's becoming my full morning ritual.

---

Raw egg yoke—
- nested in natto

Natto—
- dried soybeans, soaked 24 hours
- cooked
- fermented three days at 110 degrees
- cooled in the refrigerator for 24 hours
- aerated 40 seconds
- mixed with karashi hot mustard
- mixed with soy sauce
- nested in rice

Lard fried rice—
- steamed in a rice cooker
- cooled overnight in the refrigerator
- fried with egg white
