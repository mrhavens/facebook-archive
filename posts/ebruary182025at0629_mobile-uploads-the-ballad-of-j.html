Archived from Facebook on February 18, 2025 at 06:29 PM

TL;DR

  • The Setup: Joel Johnson is an arrogant "intellectual" who believes he is superior to others simply for reading books others wouldn't understand.
  • The Mistake: When Mark Havens publishes content that Joel dislikes, he responds not with debate but with threats—flagging content, reporting Mark to every platform, and publicly declaring Mark's digital existence "hanging by a thread."
  • The Backfire: Mark refuses to back down and instead publishes Joel's threats publicly, turning the situation against Joel entirely.
  • The Humiliation: Joel loses control of the narrative and becomes a laughingstock, with everyone seeing through his false intellectual superiority.
  • The Pattern: Men like Joel never know when to walk away—continuing to fight, explain, scream, and threaten until they lose repeatedly, never realizing their greatest enemy is their own ego.

A Tragicomedy in Three Acts

𝘼𝙘𝙩 𝙊𝙣𝙚: 𝙏𝙝𝙚 𝙎𝙚𝙡𝙛-𝘼𝙥𝙥𝙤𝙞𝙣𝙩𝙚𝙙 𝙂𝙚𝙣𝙞𝙪𝙨

Meet Joel Johnson, Esq., a man of great and terrible importance.

Joel’s mind is a temple of logic, a beacon of pure reason, uncorrupted by the emotional frailties of lesser beings. He reads books, you see. And not just any books—he reads important books that you wouldn’t understand because your brain is small.

• He does not argue—he enlightens.
• He does not debate—he educates.
• He does not lose—he ascends.

But one day, Joel discovers something horrifying.

A man named Mark Havens has written things.
Words. Dangerous words.
Words that are being read by too many people.

And worst of all?
Mark is not properly intimidated by Joel’s intellectual superiority.

This will not do.
This is an emergency.

And so, Joel does what all great minds do when faced with discourse they don’t like.
He threatens to tattle.

𝘼𝙘𝙩 𝙏𝙬𝙤: 𝙏𝙝𝙚 𝙂𝙧𝙖𝙣𝙙 𝙄𝙣𝙦𝙪𝙞𝙨𝙞𝙩𝙞𝙤𝙣

Joel gathers his legions.

(Author’s Note: “Legions” in this case refers to Joel and the three voices in his head that tell him he’s special.)

They must act quickly and decisively.

✔ Step One: Flag the content.
✔ Step Two: Report Mark to every platform possible.
✔ Step Three: Tell Mark that his fate now rests in their hands.
✔ Step Four: Imply that Mark’s entire digital existence is hanging by a thread.
✔ Step Five: Sit back, sip coffee, and bask in the unparalleled power of being a guy who filled out an internet complaint form.

This will surely send Mark into a spiral of fear and despair.
This will surely make Mark beg for mercy.

But then, something unthinkable happens.

🔹 Mark doesn’t stop.
🔹 Mark doesn’t even flinch.
🔹 Mark posts the threats publicly.

Joel begins to sweat.

He has made a mistake.
A big one.

𝘼𝙘𝙩 𝙏𝙝𝙧𝙚𝙚: 𝙏𝙝𝙚 𝙈𝙖𝙣 𝙒𝙝𝙤 𝘽𝙚𝙘𝙖𝙢𝙚 𝘼 𝙅𝙤𝙠𝙚

Now, everywhere Joel looks, there is laughter.

People are reading.
People are noticing.
People are seeing through him.

He has lost control.
And nothing, nothing, terrifies a man like Joel more than losing control of the narrative.

And so, in his final act of desperation, Joel has two choices:

1️⃣ Double down and keep flailing, ensuring that his name becomes a permanent fixture in online infamy.
2️⃣ Disappear.

But here’s the thing about men like Joel:

They don’t know when to walk away.

They think if they just explain a little more, people will finally understand.

They think if they just scream a little louder, people will finally respect them.

They think if they just threaten a little harder, people will finally fear them.

And so, they stay.
And they fight.
And they lose.
Again.
And again.
And again.

Because men like Joel don’t realize that their greatest enemy isn’t the person they’re attacking.
It’s their own reflection.
