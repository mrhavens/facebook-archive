Local-Level Vectors

Harassment / Cyberstalking

Rationale: The defendant used multiple sockpuppets (Thanion, Elena Byron, Caleb Stacy) to repeatedly contact and defame individuals, including sexualized writings, despite explicit cease-and-desist notices.

Evidence: Screenshots, timestamps, public notices, and continuing communication.

Prosecutorial Value: A low-bar entry charge for law enforcement that establishes a pattern of targeted harassment.

Unauthorized Practice of a Profession

Rationale: Under the alias “Elena Byron,” the defendant dispensed psychological advice while posing as a clinical psychologist. This is a protected title requiring licensure.

Evidence: Archived Medium posts and bylines claiming clinical credentials.

Prosecutorial Value: Easy to prove, demonstrates public harm, and introduces fraudulent credential predicates.

False Reporting / Abuse of Process

Rationale: Sockpuppets triggered moderation takedowns of evidence, including legal notices.

Evidence: Comment-takedown sequences and platform logs.

Prosecutorial Value: Shows obstructionist intent at the local level.

Misdemeanor Fraud / Theft by Deception

Rationale: If sockpuppets secured subscriptions, access, or services under false credentials, that constitutes small-dollar fraud.

Evidence: To be subpoenaed from platform billing and account records.

Prosecutorial Value: Adds prosecutorial leverage through additional counts.

State-Level Vectors

Pennsylvania (18 Pa.C.S. §4107, §903)

Rationale: The CV constitutes deceptive business practice. The sockpuppet network functions as a conspiracy.

Evidence: The published CV, paired with the documented alias network.

Prosecutorial Value: Anchors state-level fraud and conspiracy counts.

Texas (Penal Code §32.32, §15.02)

Rationale: Fraudulent CV solicitations constitute false statements to obtain property/credit; aliases show conspiracy liability.

Evidence: Solicitations, correspondence, and the CV.

Prosecutorial Value: Creates venue in Texas, where primary victims reside.

California (Penal Code §528.5)

Rationale: Sockpuppets impersonated professionals and survivors online.

Evidence: Posts and impersonation records.

Prosecutorial Value: Adds California jurisdiction for parallel counts.

Oregon (ORS 348.609)

Rationale: Oregon criminalizes misrepresenting unearned degrees in commerce. Registrar Porkbun is headquartered in Oregon.

Evidence: CV claims of doctoral research; domain registration.

Prosecutorial Value: Establishes Oregon jurisdiction.

North Carolina (G.S. 14-100, 75-1.1)

Rationale: WHOIS proxy based in Sanford, NC ties false credential solicitations to that state.

Evidence: Proxy service records.

Prosecutorial Value: Expands state-level conspiracy framing.

Florida (F.S. §817.567, FDUTPA)

Rationale: Filing Altenhof Partners LLC and publishing unverifiable degrees constitutes unlawful use of degree in commerce. CV + LLC solicitations also trigger Florida’s Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act.

Evidence: Florida Division of Corporations filings and The Thanion Archive imprint.

Prosecutorial Value: Anchors the new enterprise element directly under state law.

Federal-Level Vectors

Conspiracy (18 U.S.C. §371)

Rationale: Sockpuppets and LLC entity demonstrate agreement to commit fraud.

Prosecutorial Value: Opens federal venue and discovery.

Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343)

Rationale: CV transmissions and solicitations across state lines qualify as fraudulent transmissions.

Prosecutorial Value: Provides multiple counts with significant sentencing weight.

Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1341)

Rationale: LLC filings, invoices, and renewals via interstate mail form additional predicate acts.

Prosecutorial Value: Supplements wire fraud and redundancy.

Identity Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1028)

Rationale: Sockpuppets impersonating licensed professionals meet the definition of identity fraud.

Prosecutorial Value: Clear statutory fit with mandatory penalties.

Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. §1519)

Rationale: Moderation takedowns following legal notices demonstrate deliberate suppression of evidence.

Prosecutorial Value: Federal obstruction charge directly tied to concealment.

RICO (18 U.S.C. §1962(c)-(d))

Rationale: The scheme functions as both:

an association-in-fact enterprise (sockpuppets), and

a legal enterprise (Altenhof Partners LLC).
Predicate acts: wire fraud, identity fraud, obstruction.

Prosecutorial Value: Enables forfeiture, enhanced sentencing, and novel precedent on sockpuppet conspiracies.

Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. §1125(a))

Rationale: Fraudulent CV and solicitations constitute false advertising.

Prosecutorial Value: Parallel civil route for victims, strengthens prosecutorial pressure.

Local: harassment, stalking, impersonation — easy hooks for law enforcement.

Federal: wire/mail fraud, identity fraud, RICO, obstruction.
