You were taught to ignore your body. Not explicitly. Quietly. Systematically. You were given charts instead of trust. Calories instead of signals. Rules instead of memory. So when hunger didn’t make sense… when food never quite satisfied… when cravings escalated instead of resolved… You blamed yourself. But what if the confusion wasn’t moral? What if it was neurological? What if it was biochemical? What if your body has been speaking the entire time— …and you were trained not to listen? Because a nourished person is hard to control. They don’t panic-buy. They don’t chase constant stimulation. They don’t confuse appetite with emptiness. They sleep. They think. They remember. And remembering has always been dangerous. What we call “modern eating” isn’t progress. It’s abstraction layered on top of forgetting. Before rules, there were signals. Before pyramids, there was pattern. Before discipline, there was attunement. This isn’t about going backward. It’s about recovering a skill. One that civilization quietly made us lose— because bodies that know how to listen stop asking permission.
