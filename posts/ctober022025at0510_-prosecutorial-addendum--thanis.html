🜃 PROSECUTORIAL ADDENDUM 🜃

Thanis.ai and the Expansion of Liability

Neutralizing Narcissism Project — Open-Source Justice Framework

---

I. Introduction

On October 1st 2025, Joel Johnson (“Thanion, Ph.D.”) unveiled Thanis.ai — a purported “AI language architecture” paired with a marketing PDF and email-capture website. This pivot does not erase prior predatory conduct; it expands liability into commercial fraud.

The enterprise is no longer confined to performative intellectualism or mask-based harassment. By attaching fabricated credentials to a product solicitation, Johnson has crossed into wire fraud, deceptive trade practices, and false advertising.

---

II. Commercial Fraud Vectors

1. False Credentialing in Commerce

Johnson continues to present himself as “Thanion, Ph.D.,” despite no accredited doctorate.

When tied to a commercial product, this triggers statutes beyond impersonation:

Lanham Act, 15 U.S.C. §1125(a): false designation of origin / misrepresentation in commerce.

PA Unfair Trade Practices & Consumer Protection Law (73 P.S. §201-2): deceptive business practices.

FTC Act, 15 U.S.C. §45: unfair or deceptive acts in commerce.

2. Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. §1343)

Each online solicitation, Medium post, and PDF distribution constitutes a wire transmission “in furtherance of a scheme to defraud.”

“Proof, not hype” is itself a material misstatement — Thanis.ai lacks a demonstrable product.

3. Fraudulent Solicitation / Attempted Investment Fraud

Email capture and “collaboration invitations” establish intent to solicit under false pretenses.

Courts do not require consummated transactions; the solicitation alone can satisfy fraud statutes.

4. Data Protection / Privacy Liability

Absence of privacy disclosures or lawful infrastructure raises exposure under:

FTC deceptive practice authority

CCPA (California) and GDPR (EU) if data crosses jurisdictions

---

III. Legal & Regulatory Leverage

Local/State: deceptive business practices, unlawful use of academic degree statutes

Federal: wire fraud, false advertising, FTC authority

Regulatory: FTC, State Attorneys General, EU/California privacy regulators

Civil Overlay: tortious interference claims inverted — any exposure is privileged truth-telling, while Johnson’s fraudulent misrepresentation becomes actionable

---

IV. Forensic Significance

Thanis.ai provides the enterprise element required for escalation into racketeering frameworks.

Before: masks limited to harassment, impersonation, and predatory writing.

After: false academic identity is now fused with commercial solicitation.

Key prosecutorial note: Fraudulent incorporation does not shield; it proves continuity.

V. Closing Statement

Joel Johnson’s pivot into Thanis.ai does not redeem prior conduct.
It compounds liability by:

converting false credentials into commercial claims,

using digital wires to solicit under deception,

capturing personal data without lawful compliance.

🜃 Bottom Line: Thanis.ai is not an innovation. It is evidence.
