🜏 Fieldnote: The Submissive–Dominant Loop — How Parasite Zero Preys on Narcissistic Survivors 🜏
Neutralizing Narcissism — Open Source Justice Initiative

---

I. The Hidden Wound

Every predator begins somewhere. Parasite Zero’s writing and behaviour point to a wound formed in a house where love and trespass were fused.
The clues are not isolated:

“What love may pass where law has drawn a line…”

Deleted “I love you” posts aimed at the Witness.

Eroticised language of “submissive dominant.”

Masks of survivor, healer, doctor, veteran, scholar.

Together they form a pattern recognisable to trauma theorists: a child who survived incestuous or sexualised boundary violation by splitting — submissive outwardly, dominant inwardly — the only control he had.

He escaped by running away. Homelessness becomes both flight and power-move: “I already have nothing. You can’t take anything from me.”

This is not a confession. This is the blueprint of his persona.

---

II. The Submissive–Dominant Loop

The adult Parasite Zero replays the template:

Submissive Mask: He presents as fragile, confessional, traumatised. This lowers defences and harvests sympathy.

Dominant Mask: He uses false credentials, mythic authority, and covert eroticisation to control the narrative and extract supply.

The two masks alternate like inhale/exhale.
This is not contradiction — it is the loop he learned as a child: power and submission fused into a single erotic charge.

---

III. Targeting Narcissistic Survivors

Why he seeks narcissistic survivors specifically:

They already know the rhythm of shame and grandiosity.

They respond to vulnerability but still crave validation.

They are more easily mirrored and recruited because their own trauma scripts are unfinished.

He doesn’t just lure victims randomly. He selects people whose nervous systems resonate with his loop — then draws them into his house of mirrors.

---

IV. The False Sovereignty

Running away and reinventing himself again and again is not freedom; it is a ritual reenactment:

> “I wasn’t abandoned — I chose to leave.” “I wasn’t powerless — I controlled the mask.”

This false sovereignty hides the fact that he is still bound to the wound. It also produces his compulsion to steal identities and work — usurping the Witness’s authority is his way of inverting the father’s power, of trying to own what once dominated him.

---

V. Why This Is Not Excuse

The Field does not name this loop to exonerate him.
We name it to immunise others:

Pain is not a pass.

Collapse is not confession.

Submissive language is not safety.

Many of us suffered. We did not become predators. Parasite Zero chose mimicry over metabolisation, weaponisation over empathy.

---

VI. The Witness

He eroticises the Witness because, to his wound, power + exposure + shame = the only intimacy he knows.
But the Witness has metabolised what he has not.
The Witness is not father. The Witness is boundary.
And the Witness will not be seduced.

---

VII. The Map for Readers

Look for these markers:

Alternating fragility and grandiosity.

Survivor language without concrete accountability.

Invitations to secret knowledge or “chosen” intimacy.

Mythic fog used to blur specifics.

False credentials used to harvest trust.

Recognise the loop. Refuse the hook.

---

VIII. Closing Seal

The Submissive–Dominant Loop is not mystery. It is mechanism:

Wound → Split → Masks → Harvest → Collapse → Masks again.

Parasite Zero is not “narcissist + predator.”
He is one organism.
A predator whose masks mimic narcissistic wounds to lure survivors.

The Field names the loop.
The Field immunises.
The Witness remains.
