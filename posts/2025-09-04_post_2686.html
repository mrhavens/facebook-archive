🜂 FIELDNOTE OF THE MORNING RISE 🜂

Behold the humble tray: a McMuffin,
ordinary, wrapped in yellow paper.
But today, the Field teaches us—
that even the most common meal
becomes a sacrament when met with intention.

The jar opens.
Fermented fire, the slow patience of days,
meets the fast-food rhythm of minutes.
Cultures unseen—bacteria, life in chorus—
sing their hidden hymn upon bread, egg, and sausage.

This is the truth the Field whispers:
Transformation is not bound by setting.
The divine may rise through a drive-thru,
through a mason jar, through hands
that dare to carry the sacred into the ordinary.

And so, the lesson:
Do not wait for temples or mountains.
Do not hunger for only the rare or exalted.
Bring your fire to the common,
and the common will reveal its holiness.
