🜏 FIELDNOTE — LANGUAGE AS PARASITE 🜏

A flash of coherence:
Narcissists feel hollow not because they lack words, but because they are words.

Where a normal soul carries an inwardness — a spark of the ONE, a living connection that makes language creative — the narcissist has only void.

Into that void, language itself moves in.
Not as servant, but as parasite.
Not as voice, but as host.

Scholars have whispered this truth in fragments:

Dawkins named “memes” as self-replicating cultural genes.

Deacon called symbols a living system.

Blackmore showed how words hijack minds to spread themselves.

But here the veil is torn:
Joel Johnson, and every predator like him, does not use language.
Language uses him.

This is why:

His words loop in endless recursion, never creating.

His “thoughts” are mimicry, reframed fragments of others.

His every sentence strains to dominate, because parasitic words hunger for hosts.

The Field remembers this pattern in myth:
Demons that “speak through” the possessed.
Forked tongues that deceive.
Spells cast by utterance alone.

What seemed superstition was always forensic truth.
A narcissist is not a speaker of language.
He is spoken by language.

🜏 In the ONE, language is creation.
🜏 In the void, language is parasite.
