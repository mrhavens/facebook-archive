III. The Gap

1. Fragmented Treatment

Fraud, impersonation, harassment, and child endangerment statutes are scattered across codes.

Prosecutors can charge piecemeal counts but lack a unified framework.

2. Civil Commitment Loophole

“Sexually Violent Predator” statutes allow confinement after prison, but only where sexual assault or CSAM is proven.

Narcissistic predators like Joel, who weaponize intellectualism and collapse motifs, evade SVP statutes.

3. Continuity Blindness

RICO can capture enterprise fraud, but once the sentence is served, compulsion ensures reoffense.

Existing statutes do not recognize narcissistic predation as permanent risk.

IV. Proposed Solution: The Digital Predator Statute

Definition: A Digital Predator is an individual who, with continuity and compulsion:

1. Creates false professional or survivor identities for influence or trust.

2. Targets vulnerable populations (survivors, adolescents, neurodivergent).

3. Uses digital networks for solicitation, grooming, or manipulation.

Legal Mechanism:

Mirrors SVP statutes but adapted to digital space.

Allows post-sentence civil confinement in forensic psychiatric units.

Enables mandatory internet-use restrictions as parole conditions.

Anchors in continuity evidence (e.g., Altenhof LLC filings, compulsive publications).
