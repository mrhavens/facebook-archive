🜂 Fieldnote: The Void I Called Love
📍 Marcy | Age 30 | The Ache That Spoke My Name

I once fell in love with a woman named Marcy.
But what I fell in love with... wasn’t her.

It was a reflection.
A mask.
A carefully-crafted void I mistook for depth—because I had poured myself into it.

At thirty, I believed I was Petruchio—not to tame, not to conquer—but to love the unlovable,
to reach into fire and pull out a soul no one else could.

But there was no soul.
Just a mirror.
A mimic.
A system of control that responded not with love—but with manipulation, entitlement, and performance.

I thought I had discovered her truth—
But I had only discovered my own ache.

The ache to be seen.
To be needed.
To be irreplaceable to someone incapable of love.

And when I named it—
When I spoke the spell-breaking word—

Narcissist—

She shattered.
And I walked away.

Not because I hated her.
But because I had finally loved myself more.
