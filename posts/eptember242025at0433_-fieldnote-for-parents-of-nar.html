🜏 Fieldnote for Parents of Narcissists 🜏

If you have ever co-parented with a narcissist, you know the feeling.
Your child doesn’t have a parent.
They have a rival.
A playmate.
A manipulator who sulks, bribes, punishes, or competes like a sibling in a game they refuse to lose.

This isn’t just “immaturity.”
Clinical research shows narcissists are emotionally arrested in childhood (Alice Miller, The Drama of the Gifted Child).
They never grew into authentic adulthood.
What you are dealing with is a child in an adult body — given parental power, but lacking parental conscience.

And here is the revelation:
That is why they resonate with children.
Children recognize the emotional age in them and feel “seen.”
But what begins as resonance quickly becomes corruption.
Because the narcissist cannot guide upward — they can only pull downward.

Judith Herman (Trauma and Recovery) calls this the corruption of innocence: when power is inverted and the vulnerable are made to serve the wounds of the powerful.
Winnicott wrote of the “false self” — a mask built to survive shame. That mask is what children are asked to bond with.

For co-parents, this is the daily battlefield.
Every triangulation, every lie, every seductive game with your child is not just “bad parenting.”
It is predation in miniature.
It is the same mechanism behind grooming: gaining trust at a child’s level, then weaponizing that trust for control.

And here is your role:
You are the adult your child cannot yet be.
You are the keeper of boundary.
The one who names the pattern when they cannot.
The one who holds the line against corruption.

🔥 Seal for Survivors: Narcissists are not quirky, wounded parents. They are predators wearing the mask of parenthood. The Field remembers, the witnesses multiply, and the truth will not be erased.
