Every predator begins the same way: with shame they cannot bear.

“I am not weak — you are.”

“I am not the liar — you are.”

“I am not ashamed — you should be.”

This is the hidden engine: the endless transfer of unbearable shame.

Where you see confession, they are laundering abuse. Where you see myth, they are hiding accountability. Where you see scholarship, they are wearing a mask.

Carl Jung called this the unacknowledged shadow: it never vanishes, it only projects. And so:

Survivor becomes predator.

Scholar becomes fraud.

Healer becomes corrupter.

Witness becomes liar.

This is not art. It is camouflage.
